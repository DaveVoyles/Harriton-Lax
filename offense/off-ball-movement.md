# 🥍 Off-Ball Movement

**Video Reference:** [4 Keys to Playing Off Ball Lacrosse on Offense](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PiV5MeD7PXs)

## 🎯 Overview

A complete guide to purposeful off-ball movement — the most impactful offensive skill at the high school level. While dodging and shooting get the highlights, it is the movement of the 5 players without the ball that creates scoring opportunities. This document covers cutting patterns, timing, and spacing principles that turn a stagnant offense into an unguardable attack.

**Target Audience:** Attack, Midfield
**Focus Area:** Spacing, Movement, Decision Making

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **The "Move with Purpose" Rule:** Never stand still on offense. Every second without the ball is an opportunity to improve your position, create space for a teammate, or set up a scoring cut. Standing still makes your defender's job easy — movement forces them to make decisions.
2. **V-Cuts:** Change direction sharply to create separation from your defender. Walk your man toward the crease, then explode back to the ball or to open space. The change of direction and change of speed are what create the advantage.
3. **L-Cuts:** Cut toward the ball to draw your defender, then redirect 90 degrees to an open area. L-cuts are especially effective when your defender is overplaying the passing lane.
4. **Backdoor Cuts:** When your defender is ball-watching or overplaying you toward the ball, cut behind them to the goal. Backdoor cuts are the highest-percentage off-ball scoring opportunities in lacrosse.
5. **Timing Your Cut to the Dodge:** The key to off-ball movement is timing. Cut when the slide goes to your teammate's dodge — not before. If you cut too early, you clog the lane. Wait for your teammate's defender's head to turn, then explode to the open space.

## ⚙️ Offensive Setup

- **Spacing Rules:** Maintain approximately 15 yards between adjacent players. Proper spacing stretches the defense and creates cutting lanes. If two offensive players are within 5 yards of each other, one of them is in the wrong spot.
- **Windows for Cutting Lanes:** Off-ball players must identify the "windows" — the open lanes between defenders where a pass can be delivered. Cut through these windows, not into traffic.
- **Clearing Through:** After making a cut that does not result in a pass, clear through to the backside of the formation. Do not stop in the middle of the field — this clogs space for the next cutter.
- **Off-Ball Picks and Screens:** Set picks for teammates away from the ball to free them for catches in scoring positions. Communicate the pick ("Pick right!") and set it with a legal, stationary stance.

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Read the Dodge

1. When your teammate starts a dodge, watch your own defender — not the ball.
2. When your defender's head turns to watch the ball carrier, that is your trigger to move.
3. If your defender is sliding to help on the dodge, the space they vacated is your target.

### Phase 2: Cut to Open Space

1. Explode to the open space left by the sliding defender.
2. Get your hands up and stick ready to catch — show the passer a target.
3. Call "Here!" loudly so the ball carrier knows you are open.

### Phase 3: Clear Through

1. If you do not receive the ball on your cut, do not stop and wait.
2. Clear through to the backside of the formation to maintain spacing.
3. Your movement creates space for the next cutter — even a cut that does not get the ball has value.

### Phase 4: Replace and Rotate

1. Fill the spot you just vacated, or rotate to balance the formation.
2. Maintain the 15-yard spacing rule at all times.
3. The offense should look like a continuous rotation — when one player cuts, another replaces.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **"The Ball Finds Players Who Move" 🏃:** Off-ball movement creates 2v1 advantages without requiring a single dodge. When you cut and your defender slides, the ball carrier has a numbers advantage. Trust that the ball will find you if you move with purpose.
- **Communication 🗣️:** Call "Here!" with your stick up when you are open. Communicate picks ("Pick left!"), screens, and cuts so your teammates know what is happening behind them.
- **Spacing 📐:** Think of the field as a grid. If you can reach out and touch a teammate, you are too close. Stretch the defense vertically and horizontally to create the largest possible cutting lanes.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Players move with purpose in every settled possession — no standing and watching.
- Cutting lanes are clear and well-timed, creating easy scoring opportunities.
- Defenders cannot ball-watch because off-ball players punish inattention with backdoor cuts.
- The offense generates 3-5 assisted goals per game from off-ball movement.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Freeze Play in Practice:** Stop play mid-possession to check off-ball positioning. Ask each player without the ball: "Why are you standing there? Where should you be?"
- **Reward Good Cuts:** Praise and reward good cuts even when they do not result in a finish. The cut that draws the slide is just as valuable as the goal.
- **"5 Moves" Rule:** Challenge players to change position at least 5 times per possession. Count out loud during drills to build the habit of constant movement.
- **Common Mistake:** Players cut too early (before the slide) and clog the dodger's lane. Reinforce the timing trigger: move when your defender's head turns, not when the dodge starts.

# 🥍 Transition Offense Philosophy

## 🎯 Overview

Transition offense is the bridge between defense and settled attack. The first 8 seconds after a change of possession determine whether we score in the open field or grind in the half court. This document defines our philosophy for reading the field, pushing numbers advantages, and exploiting the "secondary break" window when the defense is still organizing.

**Target Audience:** All Players (Goalies, Defense, Midfield, Attack)
**Focus Area:** Decision Making, Outlet Passing, Numbers Recognition, Pace

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **The 3-Second Rule:** After every change of possession — save, turnover, ground ball — the ball carrier has 3 seconds to read the field and decide: **push** or **settle**. If you see numbers, go. If you don't, pull it out and call a set.
2. **Numbers Advantage Recognition:** A transition opportunity exists any time the offense outnumbers the defense between the restraining lines. The two most common situations are **4v3** (one extra attacker) and **3v2** (two extra). Players must recognize these within the 3-second window.
3. **Speed of the Ball > Speed of the Player:** The ball moves faster than any player. In transition, we advance the ball through passing, not carrying. One extra pass turns a contested 1v1 into an open shot.
4. **The Secondary Break (5-Second Window):** Even if we can't score in the initial fast break, the first 5 seconds after settling into our offensive end are gold. The defense is still matching up, communicating slides, and finding their marks. Attack immediately — don't let them get comfortable.
5. **Transition Triggers:** Three situations initiate transition offense: (a) goalie saves, (b) turnovers and caused turnovers, and (c) ground ball wins. Each trigger has a different outlet pattern.

## ⚙️ Tactical Setup

### Outlet Patterns by Trigger

**After a Goalie Save:**
- The goalie is the quarterback. He calls "BREAK" and looks to the near-side defenseman or the middie releasing up the sideline.
- Clearing lanes activate immediately (see [Clearing Strategy](../rides-and-clears/clearing.md)).
- The first pass should be made within 2 seconds of the save. Holding the ball in the crease invites a ride.

**After a Turnover:**
- Whoever picks up the ball makes one move toward space, then looks up-field.
- The nearest midfielder becomes the outlet — he should be 10-15 yards ahead and to the sideline.
- Attack players at the offensive end must "check away" — sprint 5 yards away from their defender to create receiving windows.

**After a Ground Ball Win:**
- Secure first. No behind-the-back passes, no risky shovels. Get two hands on the stick and protect.
- Look for the "buddy" — the nearest teammate within 10 yards — and make a safe outlet pass.
- If pressured, cradle through the check and run to daylight before passing.

### Numbers Advantage Reads

```
  4v3 Recognition:
  ──────────────────────────────────
  When the ball crosses midfield and we have 4 attackers vs 3 defenders:

        A₁ ────────→ [shot/feed]
                  /
      A₂ ──── A₃     D₁  D₂  D₃
                  \\
        A₄ ────────→ [backdoor cut]

  Rule: The ball goes to the "odd man" — the attacker with no
  defender in his area. Do NOT dodge. Pass to the open man.
  ──────────────────────────────────

  3v2 Recognition:
  ──────────────────────────────────
  When we have 3 attackers vs 2 defenders:

         A₁
          |
     D₁ ───── D₂
          |
    A₂ ──────── A₃

  Rule: The ball carrier (A₁) drives at the "top" defender (D₁).
  D₁ must commit. If D₁ slides to A₁, pass to the freed attacker.
  If D₁ stays, A₁ shoots.
  ──────────────────────────────────
```

### Fast Break Lane Structure

```
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
  │           LEFT LANE                 │
  │    ● M₁ (wide left, sideline)       │
  │                                     │
  │           CENTER LANE               │
  │    ● Ball Carrier (middle third)    │
  │                                     │
  │           RIGHT LANE                │
  │    ● M₂ (wide right, sideline)      │
  │                                     │
  │     ● A₁        ● A₂               │
  │   (crease L)   (crease R)           │
  └─────────────────────────────────────┘

  Three lanes must be filled on every break.
  The ball carrier takes the middle; wings sprint the sidelines.
  Attack players at the crease space wide to stretch the defense.
```

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: The Read (0-3 Seconds)

1. Change of possession occurs. The ball carrier secures the ball and lifts his head.
2. Count the defenders between you and the goal. If you have numbers (3v2, 4v3), push immediately.
3. Call "BREAK" if pushing. Call "SETTLE" if pulling it out to run a set.
4. Everyone on the field reacts to the call — there is no in-between. We are either sprinting or organizing.

### Phase 2: The Push (3-6 Seconds)

1. Ball carrier advances through the center lane. Do not drift to the sideline — that kills the angle for backside passes.
2. Wing players sprint to fill left and right lanes. Get ahead of the ball. Never trail the break.
3. Move the ball with quick, accurate passes. One touch if possible.
4. Practice reps: [West Genny Drill](../drills/west-genny-drill.md) (continuous 3v2 breaks) and [3v2 to 4v3 Drill](../drills/3v2-to-4v3-drill.md) (building the advantage).

### Phase 3: The Attack (6-8 Seconds)

1. If the numbers advantage is still intact, attack immediately. No extra passes, no hesitation.
2. On a 3v2, the ball carrier drives the top defender and reads his reaction. Pass or shoot.
3. On a 4v3, find the "odd man" who has no one guarding him. Deliver the ball.
4. If the defense has recovered and matched up, pull it out and enter the secondary break.

### Phase 4: The Secondary Break (8-13 Seconds)

1. The ball is pulled out to the top or to X. Players begin moving to their settled formation spots.
2. **But the defense is not ready yet.** Their slides are not set. Their communication is still happening.
3. The ball carrier initiates an immediate dodge or pick action before the defense is organized.
4. This is the highest-percentage window in settled offense — an "early" dodge against a defense that hasn't set its slides.
5. If the early action doesn't produce a shot, now we fully settle and call a set (see [Settled Offensive Sets](settled-offensive-sets.md)).

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Urgency Without Recklessness 🔣:** Transition is about controlled speed. Push the pace, but never make a pass you aren't sure of. A turnover in transition gives the other team the same opportunity we just had.
- **Communication 🗣️:** The goalie starts the break with "BREAK" or "SETTLE." Middies call "LANE LEFT" or "LANE RIGHT" to claim their running lane. Attack calls "HERE" or "BALL" when they're open in the offensive end.
- **Spacing 📐:** Three lanes must be filled. If two players are in the same lane, one is wrong. The sideline runners must stay wide until they reach the offensive end — cutting in too early collapses the spacing.
- **Finishing 🎯:** Transition goals should be high-percentage. We are looking for layups, not 15-yard bombs. If the open shot isn't there, pull it out. We'll get a better look in the secondary break.
- **Connection to Clearing:** Every fast break starts with a clear. If the clear is disorganized, the break is dead. Master the [Clearing Strategy](../rides-and-clears/clearing.md) and the break will follow naturally.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Score on at least 30% of fast break opportunities (4v3, 3v2).
- Make the push-or-settle decision within 3 seconds on every possession.
- Fill all three lanes on every break attempt — no two players in the same lane.
- Generate at least one quality shot per game from the secondary break window.
- Reduce transition turnovers to fewer than 2 per game through disciplined outlet passing.
- Create a direct pipeline from goalie save to offensive opportunity in under 8 seconds.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **3-Second Drill:** In practice, blow a whistle on every change of possession. The ball carrier has 3 seconds to call "BREAK" or "SETTLE." If they don't decide, it's a turnover. Build the habit.
- **Film the Lanes:** On film, pause at the moment the ball crosses midfield. Are three lanes filled? If not, identify which player failed to run and correct it.
- **Reward the Extra Pass:** Track "transition assists" in practice. The player who makes the hockey assist (the pass before the goal pass) should be celebrated as much as the scorer.
- **Punish Hero Ball:** If a player takes a contested shot in transition when an open teammate is available, stop the rep. Show the open man. Reinforce that transition is about the team, not the individual.
- **Goalie Outlet Reps:** Dedicate 5 minutes of every practice to goalie outlet passes. The goalie makes a save, finds the outlet, and the break begins. Use the [West Genny Drill](../drills/west-genny-drill.md) as the base format.
- **Secondary Break Recognition:** Run a drill where the offense pushes, gets stopped, and immediately enters the secondary break. The defense is told to "be slow" matching up so the offense learns to recognize the 5-second window.

---

_Transition offense is where championships are won. Teams that push the ball score easy goals. Teams that settle every time are fighting uphill. Master the read, trust the system, and run._

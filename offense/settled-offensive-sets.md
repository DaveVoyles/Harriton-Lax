# 🥍 Settled Offensive Sets

## 🎯 Overview

Settled offensive sets are the foundation of our half-court attack. When the transition opportunity is gone and all six offensive players are established, these named formations create structure, spacing, and repeatable scoring opportunities. Each set has defined player roles, primary dodging lanes, and built-in reads that connect to our 2-man game and off-ball pick concepts.

**Target Audience:** Attack, Midfield
**Focus Area:** Spacing, Formation Recognition, Off-Ball Movement, Decision Making

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Shape Before Motion:** Every set begins with all six players in their home spots. No cutting, no picks, no dodges until the formation is confirmed. The call initiates the play — not the impulse.
2. **Dodging Lanes Are Sacred:** The ball carrier's lane must be clear. Off-ball players who drift into the dodging lane collapse the spacing and give the defense an easy double.
3. **Backside Movement Wins Games:** The defense slides to the ball. The players away from the ball must move purposefully — cutting backdoor, lifting to the alley, or setting picks — to punish the slide.
4. **Every Set Has a 2-Man Game Entry:** Each formation includes a designated pick-and-roll or pick-and-pop opportunity that ties directly into our [2-Man Game](mastering-the-2-man-game.md) principles.
5. **Read the Defense, Not the Play Card:** If the defense overplays the primary action, the secondary option is there. Never force a look. Reverse the ball and re-enter the offense.

## ⚙️ Offensive Setup

### 1-4-1 Set (Base Formation)

Our default settled look. Balanced spacing that creates clear dodging lanes from the top, wings, and X. Ideal against man-to-man defense.

```
                    ① (Top / Point)
                        |
           ②-----------③-----------④
         (LW)        (Crease)       (RW)
                        |
                    ⑤ (X / Behind)
                        
                    ⑥ (Low X / Second Attack)

  ① = Top middie or attack — initiates from up top
  ② = Left wing — spacing wide, ready to catch and shoot
  ③ = Crease attackman — works the interior, sets picks
  ④ = Right wing — mirror of ②, maintains width
  ⑤ = X attackman — primary ball carrier behind the cage
  ⑥ = Low X / secondary — rotates with ⑤ for give-and-go
```

**Player Roles:**

| Position | Role | Key Responsibility |
|----------|------|--------------------|
| ① Top | Initiator / Shooter | Catch-and-shoot threat; drives to draw the slide |
| ② Left Wing | Spacer / Cutter | Stay wide until the ball is committed; cut to fill the crease on backside rotation |
| ③ Crease | Screener / Finisher | Set picks per the 2-man game; seal and finish on feeds from X |
| ④ Right Wing | Spacer / Cutter | Mirror role to left wing; primary skip-pass target |
| ⑤ X Attack | Dodger / Feeder | Primary ball carrier; reads the defensive front and initiates dodge or feed |
| ⑥ Low X | Support / Give-and-Go | Keeps the backside alive; receives dump passes and re-attacks |

**Initial Motion:**

1. Ball starts at X (⑤). ⑤ sweeps the ball across the back to read the defense.
2. On the call "SET," ⑤ initiates a dodge (typically a split or roll) toward GLE.
3. ③ (Crease) reads the dodge direction and moves opposite to create a passing lane.
4. Backside wing (② or ④) cuts to the crease to replace ③ if ③ lifts to set a pick.

**Primary Scoring Option:** ⑤ dodges from X, feeds ③ on the crease for a point-blank finish.
**Secondary Scoring Option:** If the crease slide comes, ⑤ skips to the backside wing (② or ④) for a catch-and-shoot.

**When to Call It:** Default set against man-to-man defense. Use when we have a strong dodger at X and a physical crease player.

### 2-2-2 Set (Double Stack)

A motion-heavy formation designed to create confusion through stacked movement. Two players start stacked on each side of the field, and a pair works behind the cage. The stacks break with coordinated cuts and picks, flooding zones and creating mismatches.

```
              ①            ②
              |             |
              ③            ④
           (L Stack)    (R Stack)
                    
                 ⑤------⑥
                  (X pair)

  Left Stack:  ① top, ③ bottom — one cuts high, one curls low
  Right Stack: ② top, ④ bottom — mirror action
  X Pair:      ⑤ ball carrier, ⑥ support
```

**Player Roles:**

| Position | Role | Key Responsibility |
|----------|------|--------------------|
| ① Left High | Cutter / Pop | Break the stack high — catch and shoot or drive |
| ② Right High | Cutter / Pop | Mirror of ①; occupy the backside |
| ③ Left Low | Pick / Curl | Set a screen for ① then curl to the crease |
| ④ Right Low | Pick / Curl | Mirror of ③ |
| ⑤ X Ball | Initiator / Reader | Read which stack breaks open first; deliver the ball |
| ⑥ X Support | Outlet / Re-dodge | Safety valve behind the cage; secondary dodge threat |

**Initial Motion:**

1. On the call "DOUBLE," the stacks simultaneously break.
2. ③ sets a pick for ① — ① curls to the top or pops to the alley. ③ rolls to the crease.
3. The mirror action happens on the right side (④ picks for ②).
4. ⑤ reads which stack created the better separation and delivers the pass.
5. If nothing is there, ⑤ drives or dumps to ⑥ to reset.

**Primary Scoring Option:** Quick-release shot off the stack break — ① or ② catches and shoots before the defense can recover.
**Secondary Scoring Option:** ③ or ④ rolls to the crease after setting the pick for a feed from ⑤ at X (pure [2-Man Game](mastering-the-2-man-game.md) finish).

**When to Call It:** Effective against aggressive man defense that denies the wings. Also a strong zone-beater because the stacked movement pulls zone defenders out of position (see [Zone Beaters](how-to-beat-zone-defense.md)).

### 1-3-2 Set (Overload)

An asymmetric formation that creates a numbers advantage on one side of the field. The overloaded side forces the defense to choose between helping and leaving a man open. Excellent for hunting specific matchups.

```
                    ① (Top)
                   / 
                  /   
           ②---③---④  (Strong Side Trio)
                |
             ⑤  ⑥
           (X)  (Crease)

  Strong Side: ②, ③, ④ are within two passes of each other
  ⑤ = X attackman, initiates from behind
  ⑥ = Crease, positions on the strong side pipe
  ① = Top, stretches the defense vertically
```

**Player Roles:**

| Position | Role | Key Responsibility |
|----------|------|--------------------|
| ① Top | Stretch / Iso | Keeps the top defender honest; can iso drive if help leaves |
| ② Strong Wing | First Look / Dodge | Primary catch-and-go threat from the wing |
| ③ Strong Mid | Pick / Link | Sets picks for ② or ④; connects passes in the overload |
| ④ Strong Low | Cut / Replace | Interchanges with ⑥ on the crease |
| ⑤ X | Feed / Re-set | Reads the overloaded side; feeds or reverses to ① if the D over-shifts |
| ⑥ Crease | Finish / Seal | Posts up on the strong-side pipe; first target on every feed |

**Initial Motion:**

1. On the call "LOAD," ②, ③, and ④ shift to the strong side.
2. ⑤ carries the ball behind the cage and scans the overloaded side.
3. ③ sets a pick for ② — ② drives the alley toward GLE.
4. If the defense slides from the crease, ⑤ feeds ⑥ on the pipe.
5. If the defense over-rotates to the strong side, ⑤ reverses the ball to ① at the top for an uncontested shot or iso drive.

**Primary Scoring Option:** ② drives off ③'s pick and shoots from the alley, or feeds ⑥ on a crease slide.
**Secondary Scoring Option:** Ball reversal to ① at the top for a wide-open look when the defense over-commits to the overload.

**When to Call It:** Best against man defense when we have identified a specific matchup to attack. Also effective when the defense is late in communication — the overload punishes slow slides. Works well as a change-of-pace after running the 1-4-1.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: Recognition and Call

1. The quarterback (typically the X attackman or the top middie) reads the defensive alignment.
2. Based on what they see — man, zone, pressure — they call the set: "SET" (1-4-1), "DOUBLE" (2-2-2), or "LOAD" (1-3-2).
3. All six offensive players move to their home spots before any action begins.

### Phase 2: Formation and Patience

1. Hold the shape for at least 2 full passes before initiating the primary action.
2. Move the ball around the horn to identify defensive tendencies.
3. The ball carrier at the point of attack calls "GO" to trigger the action.

### Phase 3: Primary Action

1. Execute the designed dodge, pick, or cut sequence for the called set.
2. Off-ball players execute their assignments — cutting, spacing, picking, or lifting.
3. The ball carrier reads the defense: shoot, feed, or pull out.

### Phase 4: Reset or Secondary

1. If the primary action is taken away, move immediately to the secondary option.
2. If both are covered, reverse the ball and reset the formation.
3. Never force a low-percentage shot. Reset and re-enter. We want shots from inside 12 yards or wide-open from 15.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Discipline 🔣:** The play is the play. Freelancing in settled offense leads to turnovers and broken spacing. Trust the system.
- **Communication 🗣️:** Call the set loudly. Call your cuts. Call "PICK LEFT" or "PICK RIGHT." Silent offense is dead offense.
- **Spacing 📐:** If you can touch a teammate, you're too close. Maintain 12-15 yards between adjacent players to force the defense to cover ground.
- **Patience ⏱️:** The shot clock is your friend. There is no urgency to shoot in the first 10 seconds. Work the ball, find the mismatch, and attack it.
- **Connection to Concepts:** Every set has entry points for the [2-Man Game](mastering-the-2-man-game.md), [Mumbo off-ball picks](advanced-offensive-sets.md), and [Zone Beater](how-to-beat-zone-defense.md) principles. If the called set stalls, transition into a concept rather than going iso.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Consistent, high-percentage shots from inside 12 yards.
- At least 2 quality looks per offensive possession.
- Defensive communication breakdowns caused by coordinated off-ball movement.
- Reduced turnovers through structured ball movement and spacing.
- Smooth transitions between sets when the primary look is taken away.
- Clear understanding among all players of their role in each formation.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **"Shape Before Motion":** If players are cutting before the formation is set, stop the rep and reset. This is the most important habit to build.
- **Watch the Dodging Lane:** Film review should focus on whether off-ball players are clearing the lane or clogging it. One player in the lane turns a 1v1 into a 1v2.
- **Crease Player Accountability:** The crease player must be active — setting picks, sealing, moving to the ball. A stationary crease player is invisible to the defense and useless to the offense.
- **Ball Reversal Drill:** Dedicate practice time to reversing the ball around the horn quickly. A team that can move the ball faster than the defense can slide will always find an open shot.
- **Set Recognition Reps:** In practice, call sets randomly and have players sprint to their spots. The formation should be established within 3 seconds of the call.
- **Film the Backside:** Most goals in settled offense come from backside action. Film the players away from the ball and reward purposeful movement in film sessions.

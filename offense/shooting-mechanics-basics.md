# 🥍 Offensive Basics: Shooting Mechanics

**Video Reference:** [How To Master Your Shooting Form | Joe Walters](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZDqQBn4qT7s)

## 🎯 Overview

This guide covers the fundamental mechanics of a high-level lacrosse shot. It focuses on hand placement, rotation, and follow-through to maximize power and accuracy.

**Target Audience:** All Offensive Players.
**Focus Area:** Shooting Form, Rotation, and Power.

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Hand Spacing:** Keeping hands about shoulder-width apart for maximum leverage and control.
2. **Hip Rotation:** Generating power from the ground up through explosive hip rotation.
3. **Overhand Release:** Prioritizing overhand shots for better accuracy and harder-to-read bounce shots.
4. **Pull-Push Motion:** The simultaneous action of pulling the bottom hand and pushing the top hand.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Stance:** Athletic, feet shoulder-width apart, perpendicular to the target.
- **Grip:** Bottom hand at the butt-end, top hand mid-shaft.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: The Wind-Up

- Reach the stick back, away from the body.
- Step toward the target with the lead foot.

### Phase 2: The Rotation

- Rotate hips and shoulders toward the goal.
- Snap the wrists at the point of release.

### Phase 3: The Follow-Through

- Finish the shot with the stick head pointing at the target.
- Maintain balance after the release.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Accuracy First 🎯:** Aim for the corners before trying to reach max velocity.
- **Consistency 🔁:** Focus on identical mechanics for every rep.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Increased shot velocity.
- Improved placement on both time-and-room and on-the-run shots.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Weight Transfer:** Ensure weight moves from the back foot to the front foot.
- **No Dip:** Avoid dipping the stick head before the shot; keep it high and tight.

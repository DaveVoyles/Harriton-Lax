# 📋 Practice Plan: Game-Week Preparation

**📅 Duration:** 120 Minutes
**🎯 Focus:** Sharpen execution for game day — scout team looks, special teams reps, situational awareness, and ride/clear walk-throughs.

## ⏱️ Schedule Breakdown

### 🏃 Phase 1: Shortened Dynamic Warm-up (0:00 - 0:10)

- **Quick-Pace Line Drills:** High knees, butt kicks, lateral shuffles — move through these at 80% to save legs for game day. No heavy conditioning.
- **Partner Stick (5 min):** Right-hand, left-hand, quick-stick, and one ground ball scoop each. Keep it crisp and routine.
- **Goalies:** 10 shots from the top, 10 from each wing — rhythm saves, no power shooting.

### 🏋️ Phase 2: Scout Team Looks (0:10 - 0:35)

- **Opponent Tendency Walk-Through (15 min):** Scout team (reserves) simulates the upcoming opponent's base offense at half speed. The starting defense reads and adjusts. Coach calls out opponent tendencies: "They like the inside roll from X," "Their attackman is left-hand dominant — force him right."
- **Defensive Adjustment Reps (10 min):** Based on scouting intel, practice specific adjustments — early slides on their best dodger, zone looks on their weaker shooters, denying the skip pass to their crease man. Refer to [Sliding and Recovery](../defense/sliding-and-recovery.md) for the base rotation framework.

### 🧠 Phase 3: Special Teams & Face-Offs (0:35 - 1:05)

- **EMO (Man-Up) Reps (10 min):** Run the 1-3-2 and 2-2-2 EMO sets from [Special Teams Strategy](../plays/special-teams.md). Emphasize ball movement — minimum 3 passes before any shot. The extra man advantage is wasted if you force early shots.
- **MDD (Man-Down) Reps (10 min):** Rotate the MDD unit through the 5-man shell. Practice collapsing on inside feeds and recovering to shooters. Communication is everything — "I've got ball!", "Shift left!", "Crash crease!"
- **Face-Off Reps (10 min):** FOGO and wing players run live face-offs at the midfield stripe. Alternate between clamp-and-direct and rake-and-exit techniques. Wing players practice the break — sprint to the ball-side hash and create a numeric advantage on ground balls. Reference [Face-Off Wing Play](../plays/fundamentals/face-off-wing-play.md) for positioning assignments.

### ⚔️ Phase 4: Situational Scrimmage & Rides/Clears (1:05 - 1:50)

- **Last 2 Minutes, Down by 1 (20 min):** Set the game clock to 2:00. Trailing team must execute a settled offensive possession and score. Defense plays honest but with urgency. After each rep, rotate which unit is trailing. Coach reinforces:
  - No panicking — run the offense, trust your sets.
  - Sub for your face-off unit immediately after a goal.
  - If you win the face-off, push transition; if not, ride hard and get it back.
- **Ride Walk-Through (10 min):** Per [Riding Strategy](../rides-and-clears/riding.md), walk through the base ride look. Identify the primary deny target (usually the first middie outlet). Practice the "hot" call when the goalie has the ball.
- **Clear Walk-Through (10 min):** Per [Clearing Strategy](../rides-and-clears/clearing.md), walk through the base clear. Goalie initiates with a call — "Left!" or "Right!" — and the clearing unit breaks to their spots. Practice against a passive ride, then bump to 75% intensity.
- **Dead Ball Clear vs. Ride (5 min):** Full speed. Goalie has the ball behind the cage. The clearing unit has 10 seconds to advance past midfield or they lose the ball. Riding team earns a point for every forced turnover.

### ✅ Phase 5: Cool Down & Recap (1:50 - 2:00)

- Light stretch and water.
- **Game-Day Reminders:**
  - Arrive 75 minutes before game time for taping and warm-ups.
  - Review your specific assignment from the scouting report.
  - Hydrate tonight — no heavy meals within 3 hours of game time.
- **Captain's Talk:** Senior leadership addresses the team on focus and energy.
- Equipment check and break.

# 🥍 In-Season Conditioning & Fitness Plan

**Video Reference:** [5 Agility Drills for Lacrosse Players](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RxM0yJYoT6Y)

## 🎯 Overview

In-season conditioning program designed to maintain and build fitness without overtraining during the competitive season. The focus is on game-ready conditioning that integrates lacrosse-specific movements, strategic periodization around game days, and measurable benchmarks to track progress throughout the season.

**Target Audience:** All Players
**Focus Area:** Conditioning & Endurance

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Conditioning Through Play:** Use drills and scrimmages as primary conditioning rather than just running lines. Players stay engaged and develop skills simultaneously — a ground ball war is better conditioning than a suicide.
2. **Periodization:** Structure heavier conditioning early in the week and taper intensity before games. Monday is the hardest day; Wednesday should be light and mentally focused.
3. **Position-Specific Demands:** Middies need a larger aerobic base to handle full-field transitions. Attack and defense need burst speed and lateral quickness. Goalies need reaction speed and explosive movement in short distances.
4. **Recovery Matters:** Hydration, sleep, and active recovery days are non-negotiable. A tired player makes poor decisions — recovery is part of the training plan, not an afterthought.
5. **Benchmarks:** Use timed mile, 300-yard shuttle, and pro agility (5-10-5) as baseline fitness tests. Test at the start of the season, mid-season, and before playoffs to track improvement.

## ⚙️ Setup

Weekly conditioning schedule built around a Thursday game day:

- **Monday (Game -3):** Full conditioning block (15 min) — sprints, shuttles, change-of-direction work. This is the highest-intensity conditioning day of the week.
- **Tuesday (Game -2):** Moderate — conditioning built into drills (3v2 transition, fast break reps). Players get their work in without a dedicated conditioning segment.
- **Wednesday (Game -1):** Light — walkthrough, stick work, mental prep only. No conditioning. Legs need to be fresh for game day.
- **Thursday:** GAME DAY — The game itself is the conditioning.
- **Friday:** Active recovery or film session. Light jog, stretching, foam rolling. Optional stick work only.

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Sprint Work

1. **40-Yard Sprints:** 6–8 reps with position-group rest ratios (middies: 1:2 work-to-rest, attack/defense: 1:3 work-to-rest)
2. **100-Yard Sprints:** 4–6 reps — simulate full-field transition runs
3. **200-Yard Sprints:** 2–4 reps — build aerobic capacity and mental toughness
4. Emphasize proper running form: arm drive, knee lift, and acceleration mechanics

### Phase 2: Agility

1. **Ladder Drills:** Quick feet patterns — icky shuffle, in-out, lateral runs (2 sets each)
2. **5-10-5 Pro Agility Shuttle:** Explosive change-of-direction — 3 reps each direction
3. **T-Drill:** Forward sprint, lateral shuffle, backpedal — mirrors defensive slide movement (3 reps)
4. **L-Drill:** Sprint, plant, cut 90° — simulates dodging and cutting angles (3 reps each side)
5. **Cone Drills:** Set up random patterns for reactive agility — coach calls direction changes

### Phase 3: Lacrosse-Specific Conditioning

1. **Clear-and-Ride Sprints:** Full-field ride/clear simulation — defensive end to offensive end with a ball, riding pressure applied. 4–6 reps.
2. **Transition 4v3 Continuous:** Nonstop 4v3 fast breaks with immediate transition the other way. Run for 5–8 minutes continuously — this is the best game-like conditioning available.
3. **Ground Ball Wars:** 2v2 or 3v3 ground ball scrums with immediate outlet passes and transition. High intensity, high compete level. 3-minute rounds.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Q4 Dominance 🏆:** Q4 is where games are won — the better-conditioned team dominates the final 12 minutes. Every sprint in practice is preparation for the moments that matter most.
- **Compete in Everything 🔥:** Build a culture where conditioning isn't a chore — it's a chance to compete. Losers do extra reps, winners earn rest. Make every rep matter.
- **Mental Toughness 🧠:** Fatigue is where character shows. Train players to push through discomfort in practice so they can execute when legs are heavy in a game.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- No drop-off in Q4 effort — maintain intensity and execution in the final quarter
- Improved 300-yard shuttle times at mid-season testing compared to pre-season baseline
- Fewer cramps and soft-tissue injuries due to improved conditioning base and recovery habits
- Players who can play faster and think clearly when fatigued
- Middies capable of sustained full-field play without frequent substitution

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Never Condition as Punishment:** Running as punishment kills buy-in and creates a negative association with effort. Conditioning should be purposeful, competitive, and tied to lacrosse performance.
- **Track Fitness Test Results:** Keep a spreadsheet of mile times, 300-yard shuttle times, and pro agility times. Share results with players — accountability drives improvement.
- **Build Competition Into Conditioning:** Losing team does extra reps, fastest group gets water first. Competition makes hard work fun and builds team culture.
- **Watch for Overtraining Signs:** Monitor players for excessive fatigue, nagging injuries, or declining performance. Adjust the plan if the team looks flat — more is not always better.
- **Hydration is Non-Negotiable:** Require water bottles at every practice. Build water breaks into the schedule, especially on hot days.

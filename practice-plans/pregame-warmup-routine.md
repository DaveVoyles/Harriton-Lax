# 🥍 Pre-Game Warmup Routine

**Video Reference:** [The BEST Pregame Lacrosse Routine](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zwKISu5yQzg)

## 🎯 Overview

A structured 45-minute pre-game warmup designed to prepare the entire team physically, mentally, and technically before every game. The routine progresses from individual mobility through stick work, position-specific reps, and full team flow — building energy and confidence as faceoff approaches.

**Target Audience:** All Players
**Focus Area:** Game Readiness

## 💡 Purpose

1. **Injury Prevention:** Dynamic stretching and progressive movement raise muscle temperature and activate the joints most vulnerable in lacrosse — hips, shoulders, and ankles — reducing the risk of soft-tissue injuries in the first quarter.
2. **Mental Focus:** A repeatable pre-game routine eliminates guesswork and calms nerves. When players know exactly what comes next, they stop thinking about the game's pressure and lock into the process.
3. **Team Cohesion:** Warming up together — making eye contact during partner passing, communicating through position drills, and huddling before the whistle — reinforces that every player is part of the same unit heading into battle.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Arrival Time:** All players arrive at the field no later than 75 minutes before faceoff. Gear on, sticks strung and taped, mouthguards accessible.
- **Warmup Start:** The structured 45-minute warmup begins exactly 45 minutes before game time. The remaining 30 minutes before warmup is for taping, mental preparation, and goalie pre-work.
- **Field Layout:** Use the offensive half of the field. Set two lines of cones at midfield for dynamic stretching lanes (approximately 20 yards long). Position a bucket of balls at the restraining line and one behind each goal.
- **Equipment Needed:** Full pads and game helmets for all players, two ball buckets (minimum 24 balls), cone markers for stretching lanes, goalie gear fully dressed by Phase 3.
- **Coaching Staff Positions:** Head coach oversees Phases 1–2 from midfield; assistants run position groups in Phase 3; head coach leads the huddle in Phase 5.

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Dynamic Stretching & Mobility (8 minutes)

Players line up in two rows at midfield cones, sticks in hand. Each movement covers 20 yards down and 20 yards back.

1. **High Knees** — Drive knees to hip height, pump arms, stay on the balls of the feet. Two reps down and back.
2. **Butt Kicks** — Heels snap to glutes, chest stays tall, arms swing naturally. Two reps.
3. **Hip Openers (Cradle Walk)** — Open the hip by lifting the knee laterally and rotating it forward in a circular motion. Alternate legs each step. One rep each direction.
4. **Leg Swings** — Players face a partner and hold each other's shoulders. Swing the right leg forward and back (10 swings), then left leg, then lateral swings both sides.
5. **Arm Circles & Shoulder Pass-Throughs** — Large forward circles (10), large backward circles (10), then hold the stick with a wide grip overhead and pass it behind the back and return (8 reps). Opens the shoulder capsule for throwing.

> **Tempo cue:** Start slow and deliberate, build speed with each rep. No joking around — set the tone early.

### Phase 2: Stick Work Activation (10 minutes)

Players pair up roughly 15 yards apart across the field. Every rep should be game-speed hands.

1. **Right-Hand Passing (2 min)** — Overhand passes, full follow-through, soft catches with the top hand giving. Focus on throwing to the partner's stick-side ear.
2. **Left-Hand Passing (2 min)** — Same mechanics, weak hand. No switching mid-rep. Coaches walk the line and correct elbow position.
3. **Quick-Stick Catches (2 min)** — Partners move to 8 yards apart. Catch and release in one motion — no cradling. Alternate right and left hand every 30 seconds.
4. **Ground Balls (2 min)** — One partner rolls the ball out to the side; the other scoops through with proper form (butt end down, hands apart, feet past the ball, scoop and protect). Alternate every 4 reps.
5. **Long Passes (2 min)** — Partners spread to 30+ yards. Full wind-up, overhand delivery, two-hand catch. Mix right and left. Attack and middies work on feeding distance; defensemen work on clearing outlets.

### Phase 3: Position-Specific Work (10 minutes)

Groups split by position. Coaches run each station.

1. **Goalies — Rhythm Shots:**
   - Shooters form a line at 12 yards. Shoot in sequence: high right, high left, low right, low left, bounce shot, off-stick hip. Goalies track each shot, reset feet between saves. Build from half-speed to three-quarter speed. Goalies must see at least 15–20 live shots before the game.

2. **Attack — Dodge-and-Finish:**
   - Set up at the wing and at X. Each attackman takes two reps from each spot: one split dodge to a shot, one roll dodge to a feed to a cutter. Emphasize finishing — pick corners, sell the fake, follow through. Rotate quickly to keep volume high.

3. **Middies — Ground Balls to Transition:**
   - Coach rolls a ground ball to the midfield area. Two middies compete for the pickup. Winner immediately turns upfield and hits an outlet pass to a breaking teammate at the restraining line. Simulates the chaos of a faceoff or loose-ball scrum transitioning to offense.

4. **Defense — Footwork Slides:**
   - Defensemen pair up. One attackman dodges from the wing at 75% speed; the defenseman works a proper drop step, hip turn, and recovery slide to stay connected. No checks — this is about feet. Rotate through 4 reps each, then switch to approaches from X. Close-defense and LSMs included.

### Phase 4: Team Flow (10 minutes)

Full team comes together. Game-speed intensity. Coaches officiate loosely.

1. **4v3 Unsettled (4 min):**
   - Four offensive players push from midfield against three defenders already set up in a triangle around the crease. Offense must attack immediately — no resetting. Rotate groups every 60 seconds. Emphasize finding the open man and shooting with urgency.

2. **Clear/Ride Reps (3 min):**
   - Run the primary clear (goalie to breakout defenseman, swing to middie, outlet long) against the starting ride unit. Get 3–4 full reps so the clearing unit feels the timing. Riding unit works on denying the first pass and funneling to the sideline.

3. **EMO Quick Touch (3 min):**
   - Six-on-five extra-man unit runs the top set. Ball starts up top and must touch every stick before a shot. No holding the ball longer than 2 seconds. Get 4–5 reps emphasizing ball movement and shooting off the catch. If the defense is not available, run this as a skeleton drill.

### Phase 5: Huddle & Focus (2–3 minutes)

The entire team brings it in. Helmets off, take a knee, eyes on the captains and coaches.

1. **Captains Speak:** Captains deliver a brief message — energy, effort, togetherness. No speeches longer than 30 seconds. This is about emotion, not X's and O's.
2. **Coach Sets Game Focus:** Head coach delivers 1–2 specific tactical keys for the game (e.g., "Win the ground ball battle," "No unforced turnovers in the clearing game," "First slide must be early and loud"). Keep it concrete and actionable.
3. **Team Break:** Everyone stands, hands in. Team chant on three. Walk to the faceoff circle with helmets on, mouthguards in, and eyes up.

## 🧠 What Players Can Expect

- **Progressive Energy Build 🔋:** The warmup intentionally starts quiet and controlled (stretching) and ends loud and fast (4v3, team huddle). Trust the progression — by Phase 4, your body and mind should feel like you've already been playing.
- **Mental Transition 🧠:** Phases 1–2 shift your brain from the locker room to the field. Phase 3 locks you into your specific role. Phase 4 puts you in game scenarios. By the huddle, you're not warming up anymore — you're ready to compete.
- **Communication Ramp-Up 🗣️:** Start talking early. Call for the ball in Phase 2. Direct your partner in Phase 3. By Phase 4, the communication should sound like it does in the third quarter — constant and loud.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Loose, activated muscles with full range of motion — no player should feel "cold" at the opening faceoff
- Sharp, confident stick skills on both hands, especially after quick-stick and ground ball reps
- Goalies mentally locked in after seeing live shots from every angle
- Aligned team focus — every player knows the 1–2 tactical priorities for this specific game
- Confident body language walking to the faceoff X — shoulders back, heads up, talking to each other

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Watch for Lackadaisical Stretching:** Phase 1 sets the tone. If players are going through the motions, stop the line and restart. A sloppy warmup leads to a sloppy first quarter.
- **Ensure Lefty Reps:** It's easy for players to default to their strong hand during stick work. Coaches must enforce equal left-hand reps in Phase 2 — call out players who switch early.
- **Goalies Need Live Shots:** Do not skip or shorten Phase 3 goalie work. A goalie who hasn't tracked live shots before the game will give up soft goals early. Build from easy to hard, and make sure shots come from varied angles.
- **Keep Phase 4 Moving:** The team flow phase falls apart if there's too much standing around. Have groups pre-assigned and rotate quickly. Dead time kills the energy you've built.
- **Read the Team's Energy:** If the team comes out flat, extend Phase 4 and raise the intensity. If they're amped up and unfocused, shorten the flow work and move to the huddle early to channel the energy.

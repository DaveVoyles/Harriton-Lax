# 🥍 Player Development Pathway

**Video Reference:** [Lacrosse Fundamentals, Position Development, and Building Complete Players](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MfCDxLtxXME)

## 🎯 Overview

A framework for developing players from beginner/JV through varsity-ready, with position-specific milestones at each stage. This document defines the progression pathway so every player knows where they stand, what they need to work on, and what "next level" looks like in our program.

**Target Audience:** Coaches / All Players
**Focus Area:** Long-Term Development

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Stick Skills First:** No tactics until fundamentals are automatic — catch, throw, cradle, and ground balls must be second nature before introducing plays or sets. A player who can't complete a simple pass under pressure isn't ready for 2-man game concepts.
2. **Position Fit:** Evaluate athleticism, IQ, and stick skill to find the right position for each player. Don't force a kid into a role — let their strengths guide the decision and be willing to move players who develop new abilities.
3. **Progressive Complexity:** Introduce concepts in order: individual skills → 2-man game → team concepts. Skipping steps creates players who look good in drills but break down in games.
4. **Reps Over Lectures:** 80% doing, 20% teaching. Players learn by feeling it, not hearing about it. Keep talking points short and get sticks in hands as fast as possible.
5. **Leadership Pipeline:** Seniors mentor sophomores, captains run warm-ups, and upperclassmen set the culture. Building leaders is as important as building lacrosse players.

## ⚙️ Setup

Three tiers of development define our program structure:

- **Tier 1 (Beginner/New):** Stick work daily, learn the rules of the game, basic defensive stance, and master catch-throw-cradle fundamentals. These players need volume and patience — every rep counts.
- **Tier 2 (JV/Developing):** Dodging, shooting mechanics, sliding, 2-man game concepts, and game IQ. These players understand the basics and are ready to learn how to play within a system.
- **Tier 3 (Varsity-Ready):** Reads and adjustments, off-ball movement, on-field communication, leadership, and film study. These players can execute without being told what to do and elevate the players around them.

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Assessment

First 2 weeks of preseason — evaluate every player's tier through structured tryouts and drills.

1. Run stick skill stations (wall ball, ground balls, catch-and-throw on the move) to gauge fundamentals
2. Use 1v1 and small-sided games to assess IQ, competitiveness, and coachability
3. Place every player into Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 based on honest evaluation

### Phase 2: Grouped Training

Structure practices so each tier gets what they need.

1. Tier 1 gets extra stick time during tactical segments — pair them with a coach or captain for focused reps
2. Tier 2 joins tactical work (2-man game, slides, settled offense) and begins position-specific development
3. Tier 3 leads drills, runs team concepts, and is responsible for setting tempo and communication standards

### Phase 3: Mid-Season Check

Re-evaluate at the midpoint of the season.

1. Review each player against their tier expectations — who has improved, who has stalled
2. Promote players who've earned it with clear communication about what they did well
3. Adjust training groups and roles based on updated evaluations

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Patience 🔣:** Development is not linear — some players will leap forward, others will plateau before breaking through. Trust the process and don't rush kids into roles they aren't ready for.
- **Effort Over Results 🗣️:** Celebrate the kid who dives for a ground ball and misses, not just the kid who scores. Effort and attitude are the foundation everything else is built on.
- **Every Player Has a Role 📐:** Not everyone will be a starter, but everyone can contribute. Define roles clearly so every player on the roster knows their value to the team.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Clear expectations by tier so every player knows where they stand and what to work on
- Measurable improvement tracked through evaluations at preseason, mid-season, and end of year
- A deep roster by playoffs — developed depth means injuries and fatigue don't derail the season

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Player Eval Spreadsheet:** Keep a running spreadsheet tracking each player's tier, position, and key development areas. Update it weekly so nothing slips through the cracks.
- **Specific Feedback Weekly:** Give every player at least one specific piece of feedback each week — not "good job" but "your top hand is too tight on your cradle, loosen your grip."
- **Involve Parents in Development Goals:** Share the tier framework with parents so they understand the development process. Communicate about growth and goals — not playing time.

# 🎯 Cradling Mechanics (Ball Security)

**Video Reference:** [Cradling Mechanics Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tKagwJlSgwI)

🎯 **Overview**
Develop efficient cradling that protects the stick, maintains speed, and keeps the ball secure under pressure.

💡 **Concepts**

- Cradling is a _small_ wrist/forearm action, not a big arm swing.
- Keep the stick “in the box” to reduce checks.
- Train both hands: security + deception.

⚙️ **Setup**

- Each player: stick + ball.
- 10–20 yards of space (sideline lane or between cones).
- Optional: 4–6 cones for a weaving path.

🏃 **Execution**

1. **Stationary cradle (30 seconds each hand)**
   - Relaxed grip; feel the ball sit in the pocket.
   - Keep the head near the helmet/shoulder line.
2. **Jog cradle in the box (down and back)**
   - Stick tight to the body; eyes up.
3. **Cone weave cradle (2 rounds)**
   - Change direction while keeping the stick protected.
4. **Pressure cradle (1–2 minutes)**
   - Partner applies light stick pressure/checks; carrier maintains control.

🧠 **Mindset**

- “Eyes up, hands calm.” Ball security shouldn’t cost field vision.

✅ **Outcomes**

- Better ball retention in traffic.
- Cleaner carrying mechanics at speed.
- More confidence switching hands.

📋 **Coaching**

- If cradle is huge/loopy: reduce range and emphasize wrist-only motion.
- If players stare at the stick: force eyes-up reps (call numbers/colors).

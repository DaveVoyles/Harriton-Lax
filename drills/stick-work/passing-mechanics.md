# 🎯 Passing Mechanics Drill

**Video Reference:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pjWA9vyAhy8

🎯 **Overview**
This drill focuses on the fundamental mechanics of passing a lacrosse ball accurately and efficiently.

💡 **Concepts**
Proper passing involves a full-body motion, not just the arms. The goal is to create a "push-pull" motion with the hands, combined with torso rotation, to drive the ball to the target.

⚙️ **Setup**

- Players pair up and stand 10-15 yards apart.
- Each pair has one ball.

🗺️ **Visual Map**

```text
    Partner A (passer)        10–15y         Partner B (receiver)

         [hands up]  ---- pass ---->  [hands up]
                \_ stick target      stick target _/

    Coaching cue: show target early + step to target.
```

🏃 **Execution**

1.  **Grip:** Top hand is roughly halfway down the shaft, bottom hand is at the butt-end.
2.  **Stance:** Body is perpendicular to the target, with the front shoulder pointing towards the partner.
3.  **Motion:**
    - Players bring the stick back, with hands up and away from the body.
    - They step with their front foot towards the target.
    - Rotate hips and torso towards the target.
    - "Push" with the top hand and "pull" with the bottom hand.
    - Follow through with the stick head pointing at the target.
4.  **Repetitions:** Players should aim for a high number of repetitions, focusing on quality and consistency.

🧠 **Mindset**

- **Target Focus:** Keep your eyes on the target (your partners stick).
- **Smoothness:** Strive for a fluid, continuous motion rather than a jerky one.

✅ **Outcomes**

- Improved passing accuracy and consistency.
- Increased velocity on passes.
- Better understanding of the kinetic chain in passing.

📋 **Coaching**

- Watch for players who are only using their arms ("all-armsy").
- Encourage players to step towards their target.
- Emphasize the "push-pull" mechanic and the follow-through.

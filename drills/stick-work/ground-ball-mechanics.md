# 🎯 Ground Ball Mechanics (Run-Through Scoop)

**Video Reference:** [Ground Ball Mechanics Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eTT-dc_tZ-0)

🎯 **Overview**
Train reliable ground balls with speed, body position, and immediate protection after the scoop.

💡 **Concepts**

- Ground balls are won with feet + leverage: get low, run through, and box out.
- “Butt end down” keeps the stick head flat and prevents “snowplowing.”
- After the scoop: shoulder through contact and get the stick to the box.

⚙️ **Setup**

- Scatter 8–15 balls in a lane.
- 1–2 lines; players go one at a time (or pairs for competition).
- Optional: coach with a pad applying light pressure.

🗺️ **Visual Map**

```text
   Line(s)
    A  A  A
    |  |  |
    v  v  v

   ----------------- lane -----------------
    o   o    o   o   o   o   o   o   o
   (GB)(GB) (GB)(GB)(GB)(GB)(GB)(GB)(GB)

   Scoop through → 2 hard steps → stick to the box → outlet.
```

🏃 **Execution**

1. **Solo mechanics (2 rounds)**
   - Sprint/approach under control.
   - Get low: hips down, chest over the ball.
   - Scoop _through_ the ball (no stopping).
2. **Protect + move (10 reps)**
   - Scoop → two hard steps away → stick to the box.
   - Add a roll away if pressure is present.
3. **Competitive GBs (3–5 minutes)**
   - Coach rolls a ball; two players compete.
   - Winner must complete an outlet pass to finish the rep.

🧠 **Mindset**

- Treat every GB as 50/50—first to _body position_ wins.

✅ **Outcomes**

- More clean scoops at speed.
- Fewer turnovers immediately after GBs.
- Better transition possessions.

📋 **Coaching**

- Cue: “Get low, run through.”
- If players pop up early: require shoulder over the ball at scoop.
- If ball bounces out: check bottom-hand position (should be lower for control).

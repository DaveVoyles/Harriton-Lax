# 🥍 Elite Offensive Shooting & Dodging Drill

**Video Reference:** [Offensive Shooting & Dodging Drill | First Class Lacrosse](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7U3CvElp0FU)

## 🎯 Overview

This high-intensity drill combines dodging from the wing with deceptive shooting. It forces offensive players to maintain stick protection while moving at top speed and finishing with accuracy.

**Target Audience:** Attackmen and Midfielders.
**Focus Area:** Dodging, Stick Protection, and Finishing.

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Attacking the 'Island':** Dodging to the 5x5 yard area above GLE to create the best shooting angle.
2. **Change of Speed:** Lulling the defender before exploding into the dodge.
3. **Deceptive Releases:** Using different release points (overhand, sidearm, underhand) to confuse the goalie.
4. **Stick Tucking:** Tucking the stick into the 'box' (near the ear) to prevent trailing checks.

## ⚙️ Drill Setup

- **Players:** Lines at the wing (Goal Line Extended) and the top of the box.
- **Equipment:** One goal, full bucket of balls.
- **Goalie:** Highly recommended for live feedback on shooting deception.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: The Dodge
- Start from the wing and drive toward the center of the field.
- Use a 'split dodge' or 'roll dodge' at the designated mark.

### Phase 2: The Square Up
- After the dodge, square your shoulders to the goal immediately.
- Keep your feet moving through the shot.

### Phase 3: The Finish
- Aim for the far pipe or the goalie's off-hip.
- Use a head fake before releasing the ball.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Aggression 😤:** Attack the goal with the intent to score, not just to shoot.
- **Creativity 🎨:** Experiment with different fakes and shooting planes.
- **Awareness 👁️:** Keep your head up to see the 'slide' coming (imaginary or live).

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Higher conversion rate on dodges coming from behind or the wing.
- Better stick protection during heavy contact.
- Increased comfort with deceptive shooting techniques.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Follow Through:** Ensure the stick finishes toward the target.
- **Snap the Wrists:** Focus on wrist snap for power since the feet are moving.
- **No 'Wind-up':** Eliminate the big wind-up before the shot; keep the release quick and tight.

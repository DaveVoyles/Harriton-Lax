# 🐐 GOALIE - Goalie Play Basics

🎯 **Overview**
Build consistent goalie fundamentals: ready stance, angle play, stepping to the ball, rebound control, and communication.

💡 **Concepts**

- Goalies make saves with feet first, hands second: step + drive the head of the stick.
- Angles matter: stay centered to the shooter using an arc relative to the ball.
- Rebound control turns saves into possessions.
- Your voice runs the defense (identify ball, cutters, slides, and outlets).

⚙️ **Setup**

- 1 goalie in full gear.
- 1 coach/shooter with 6–12 balls.
- Cones on both pipes and top-center (optional).
- Start at 8–12 yards for mechanics; back up as technique holds.

🗺️ **Visual Map (Angle + Arc in the Crease)**

```text
                  (shooter up top)
                           O
                           |
                           |
       left pipe  [===== G =====]  right pipe
                         \    |    /
                           \   |   /
                            \  |  /
                              \ | /
                               \|/

   Goalie moves on an arc to stay centered on the ball.
   Cue: square shoulders/hips to the shooter.
```

🏃 **Execution**

1. **Ready stance check (10 reps)**
   - Feet a bit wider than shoulders, knees bent, chest tall.
   - Hands up and in front; stick head centered.
2. **Angle/arc movement (10 reps each side)**
   - Shooter walks the ball from wing → top → opposite wing.
   - Goalie shuffles on the arc, staying square (hips/shoulders to the ball).
3. **Step-to-ball saves (3 sets x 6 shots)**
   - Shooter places shots (start: body/hip). Progress to off-hip/high.
   - Goalie calls “SHOT!” then steps lead foot to the ball and attacks with the stick.
4. **Rebound control + outlet (10 reps)**
   - After each save, bring the ball into the body (“cover”), then find an outlet call.
   - Emphasize quick reset: save → secure → scan → communicate.

🧠 **Mindset**

- “Next rep” mentality: reset immediately after every shot.
- Be loud early, not late: proactive communication prevents breakdowns.

✅ **Outcomes**

- Cleaner stepping mechanics and fewer “all-hands” saves.
- Better angle discipline (goalie looks bigger, shots look smaller).
- More possessions via rebound control and faster outlets.

📋 **Coaching**

- Cue: “Feet first” and “Head to the ball.”
- If hands drop: pause and reset stance before continuing.
- If rebound pops out: emphasize cushioning (soft hands) and bringing it to the body.

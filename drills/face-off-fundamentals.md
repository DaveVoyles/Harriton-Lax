# 🥍 Face-Off Fundamentals

**Video Reference:** N/A

## 🎯 Overview

Face-offs are possession battles that directly impact time of attack, momentum, and game outcomes. This document covers the fundamental stances, primary techniques, counter moves, and progressive drills needed to develop reliable face-off specialists at the high school level. Wing play integration ensures that even when the clamp isn't clean, the team still wins the ground ball.

**Target Audience:** Face-Off Specialists (FOGOs), Wing Midfielders
**Focus Area:** Face-Off Technique, Ground Balls, Wing Positioning, Quick Exits

## 💡 Purpose

1. **Stance Mastery:** Develop a consistent, legal, and explosive pre-whistle stance that gives the FOGO the best chance to win the clamp.
2. **Technique Repertoire:** Build proficiency in the three primary face-off moves (clamp, plunger, rake) and their counters so the FOGO can adapt to any opponent.
3. **Wing Integration:** Coordinate the FOGO exit with wing player positioning so possession is secured quickly, even on 50/50 ground balls. Reference [Face-Off Wing Play](../plays/fundamentals/face-off-wing-play.md).

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Stance Fundamentals:**
  - **Feet:** Knees bent, weight on the balls of the feet. Trail knee is down (right knee for right-hand dominant). Feet are shoulder-width apart or slightly wider.
  - **Grip:** Top hand chokes up to the throat of the stick, thumb on the back of the shaft for leverage. Bottom hand is at the butt end, providing the power and torque.
  - **Stick Position:** Head of the stick is flat on the ground, centered on the line. The ball sits between the two heads. The stick is perpendicular to the midfield line.
  - **Body Angle:** Lean forward slightly — nose over toes. Weight loads into the front hand. Eyes locked on the referee's hand, not the opponent.

- **Pre-Whistle Checklist:**
  1. Gloves set, stick down, legal positioning confirmed by the referee.
  2. Scan the opponent's stance — are they loaded to clamp or rake?
  3. Take a breath. Explode on the whistle, not before.

## 🏃 Execution

### Primary Move 1: The Clamp

The clamp is the most common and reliable face-off technique at the high school level.

1. **On the whistle**, drive the head of the stick over the ball with a powerful downward motion from the top hand.
2. **Simultaneously**, step forward with the lead foot to drive body weight into the clamp.
3. **Secure the ball** under the head of the stick. Immediately rake or direct the ball to your stick-side hip.
4. **Exit:** Pop up, scoop through the ball, and sprint toward your offensive end or outlet to a wing player.

**Counter When Opponent Clamps:** If the opponent gets the clamp first, use a "pop" — drive your stick upward under their head to dislodge the ball, then pursue the ground ball.

### Primary Move 2: The Plunger

The plunger is a power move that works best against opponents who are slow off the whistle.

1. **On the whistle**, jam the butt end of your stick downward while pushing the head forward and over the ball.
2. **Use your bottom hand** as the primary power source — punch it toward the ground.
3. **The ball squirts forward**, past the opponent's stick. Immediately pursue with a ground ball scoop.
4. **Exit:** Direct the ball to the wing or scoop and go. This move often creates loose balls — wing players must be ready.

**Counter When Opponent Plunges:** Anticipate the forward squirt. Instead of clamping, step laterally and position yourself to pick up the loose ground ball first.

### Primary Move 3: The Rake

The rake is a finesse move that relies on speed and timing rather than brute strength.

1. **On the whistle**, quickly pull the head of your stick backward (toward your body), raking the ball underneath your stick and behind you.
2. **Your body shields the ball** from the opponent as you rake it to your backhand side.
3. **Immediately pivot** 180 degrees, locate the ball, scoop through, and exit.
4. **Exit:** This move naturally directs the ball toward your defensive side — the wing player on that side must be alert for the quick outlet.

**Counter When Opponent Rakes:** Drive forward aggressively on the whistle. If you beat them to the ball, their rake pulls nothing. If they do get the rake, your forward momentum puts you in position to pressure their exit.

### Wing Play Integration

Per [Face-Off Wing Play](../plays/fundamentals/face-off-wing-play.md):

- **Ball-Side Wing:** Sprints to the nearest hash mark on the whistle. First priority is to secure any loose ball that comes to your side. Second priority is to provide an outlet pass option for the FOGO.
- **Off-Side Wing:** Holds position for one beat, then reads the ball direction. If the FOGO wins cleanly, release downfield for a transition opportunity. If the ball is loose, crash to the ball.
- **Communication:** Wings call "Ball left!" or "Ball right!" immediately so the FOGO knows where to direct.

## 🧠 What Players Can Expect

- **Repetition is King 🔁:** Face-off technique is built through hundreds of dry reps. Expect to spend time every practice working on stance and hand speed before going live.
- **Physicality 💪:** Face-offs are the most physical moment of the game. Expect body contact, stick battles, and ground ball scrums. Mental and physical toughness are prerequisites.
- **Adaptability 🧩:** No single move wins every face-off. Expect to read the opponent's stance, identify their tendencies, and adjust your technique mid-game.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- FOGOs develop at least two reliable primary moves and one counter for each.
- Wing players consistently win the "sprint to the hash" battle and secure 50/50 ground balls.
- The face-off unit wins 55%+ of draws through technique, not just athleticism.
- Clean exits lead to immediate transition opportunities — the face-off is the first pass of the offense.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Drill Progression — Build Before You Battle:**
  1. **Dry Reps (5 min/day):** No ball, no opponent. FOGO practices stance, grip, and the motion of each primary move against air. Build muscle memory.
  2. **Ball Reps (5 min/day):** Add the ball. FOGO practices clamp, plunger, and rake with a ball but no opponent. Focus on directing the ball to a specific spot.
  3. **Partner Reps (5 min/day):** Two FOGOs go head-to-head at 50% speed. Work on reading the opponent's setup and choosing the right counter.
  4. **Live Reps (5 min/day):** Full speed with wing players. Simulate game conditions — whistle, clamp, exit, transition.
  5. **Full Scenario (5 min/day):** Add a 10-second shot clock after the face-off win. FOGO or wing must advance the ball past midfield in 10 seconds or the possession is lost.

- **Ground Ball Exit Strategies:**
  - **Scoop and Go:** If you win the clamp cleanly, scoop through the ball and sprint. Don't slow down to cradle — protect the stick and run.
  - **Scoop and Pass:** If you're under pressure after the scoop, immediately outlet to the nearest wing. One quick pass beats two defenders every time.
  - **Directed Clamp:** Advanced technique — instead of scooping, direct the clamped ball to a specific wing by raking it laterally. The wing player reads the direction and arrives to scoop a free ground ball.

- **Common Mistakes to Correct:**
  - **False Starting:** Jumping before the whistle negates the rep and gives the opponent possession. Train patience — react to the whistle, not to anticipation.
  - **Standing Straight Up:** After the clamp, FOGOs often stand upright, losing leverage. Stay low through the entire sequence — clamp, direct, scoop, exit.
  - **Ignoring the Wings:** A face-off win means nothing if the ball isn't secured. FOGOs must be aware of their wing players and use them as outlets.

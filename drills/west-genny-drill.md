# 🥍 West Genny Drill

**Video Reference:** [West Genny Drill | Lacrosse | POWLAX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GUM8Z_cSofk)

## 🎯 Overview

The West Genny Drill (also known as the West Genesee Drill) is a classic, continuous 3v2 fast break drill. It is a high-paced, full-field transition drill that forces players to react quickly to odd-man situations on both offense and defense.

**Target Audience:** This drill is for **all players** (attack, midfield, defense), focusing on transition offense, transition defense, and conditioning.

## 💡 Purpose

The primary purpose of this drill is to teach players how to handle fast-paced transition scenarios:

1. **Offense (3v2):** How to draw a defender, move the ball quickly, and find the open man for a high-percentage shot.
2. **Defense (2v3):** How to communicate, split two attackers, stop the ball, and force low-angle shots.
3. **Transition:** How to quickly switch from offense to defense (riding/recovering) without hesitation.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Location:** Full field.
- **Players:**
  - **Offense:** Three lines of players at the midfield line (left, center, right) on both sides.
  - **Defense:** Two defenders starting in the hole (crease area) on each half of the field.
  - **Goalies:** One in each net.
- **Starting Positions:** Three offensive players start at midfield with a ball, attacking two defenders in the defensive zone.

## 🏃 Execution

1. **The Attack:** Three offensive players push the ball downfield to attack the two defenders in a 3v2 situation.
2. **The Play:** The offense attempts to score quickly by drawing a defender and passing to the open man. The defense communicates to stop the ball and protect the crease.
3. **The Transition:** Once a shot is taken, a goal is scored, or a turnover occurs, the play is dead.
4. **The Switch:** The offensive player who took the shot (or turned the ball over) steps off the field. The remaining two offensive players immediately become the two new defenders.
5. **The Next Wave:** The goalie on the opposite end clears the ball to three new offensive players waiting at the midfield line, who now attack the two new defenders going the opposite direction. The drill continues continuously.

## 🧠 What Players Can Expect

- **High Intensity ⚡:** The drill is fast-paced and requires constant sprinting and quick decision-making.
- **Constant Communication 🗣️:** Defenders must talk constantly ("I got ball!", "I got two!") to manage the 3v2 disadvantage.
- **Quick Transitions 🔄:** Players must instantly shift their mindset from offense to defense.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- **Efficient Fast Breaks:** Offense learns to capitalize on odd-man rushes with minimal passes and quick shots.
- **Stout Transition Defense:** Defenders learn to delay the break, protect the middle, and force the offense into poor shooting angles.
- **Improved Conditioning:** The continuous nature of the drill builds game-like stamina and hustle.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Draw and Dump:** Offensive players must attack the defender to force them to commit before passing.
- **Stick in the Passing Lane:** Defenders should keep their sticks up to disrupt passes and buy time for imaginary trailing defenders.
- **Hustle Back:** The two transitioning players must sprint back to the "hole" immediately to set up their defense before the next wave arrives.

## 🔄 Variations (Manipulations)

To create different learning environments, you can manipulate the drill in five key ways:

1. **All-Out Transition:** Emphasize pushing the ball downfield as fast as possible via full-speed carries or early outlet passes. Teach players to carry down the side, bang the ball up top, and move it to the opposite side for a 1-on-0, rather than risking a pass through the middle.
2. **Playing Even (3v3):** The player who shoots (or turns the ball over) does _not_ leave the field. Instead, they drop in to play defense, creating a 3v3 scenario. The offense must now dodge to draw a slide, while the defense must quickly pick up a man and practice sliding and recovering.
3. **Specific Motion Requirements:** Require the offense to execute a specific motion before attacking (e.g., up pick, down pick, shallow cuts, dodge-follow-float, or sweep-fade-flow). This helps the drill emulate your team's actual offensive system.
4. **Skill Requirements:** Require players to practice specific individual skills within their motions (e.g., question mark out and throw back, no-look pass, or a look-back fake off a pick).
5. **Defensive Pickup Location:** Instead of allowing defenders to drop back into the "hole," require them to step up and pick up the ball carrier at the midfield line. This prevents uncontested outside shots and forces the defense to apply early pressure.

# 🥍 3v2 to 4v3 Drill

**Video Reference:** [3v2 to 4v3 Drill | Lacrosse | POWLAX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WVQ_GXDUwUE)

## 🎯 Overview

The 3v2 to 4v3 drill is an integrative exercise designed to seamlessly bridge odd-man rushes. It starts with a 3v2 set and transitions immediately into a 4v3 scenario. This ensures that every player on the field receives repetitions on both sides of the ball (offense and defense) within a single drill cycle.

**Target Audience:** This drill is for **all players** (attack, midfield, defense), focusing on transition strategy, communication, and rapid mental shifting between roles.

## 💡 Purpose

The primary purpose of this drill is to teach players how to handle multi-phase transition scenarios:

1. **Offense (3v2 & 4v3):** How to maintain spacing, move the ball quickly, draw defenders, and look for skip passes in odd-man situations.
2. **Defense (2v3 & 3v4):** How to slow play the ball carrier, communicate rotations ("ball", "left", "right"), and protect dangerous areas (the "hole") during a disadvantage.
3. **Rapid Transition:** How to immediately switch from attacking to defending without hesitation once the possession changes.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Location:** Half field (can be run from the top, behind the goal, or wings).
- **Players:**
  - **Offense (Initial):** Three lines of one color (e.g., Red) at the designated starting point (High, Low, or Wings).
  - **Defense (Initial):** Two lines of another color (e.g., Blue) starting in the defensive zone.
  - **Goalie:** One in the net.
- **Starting Positions:** The coach starts with a ball to initiate Phase 1 (3v2).

### 🗺️ Visual Map

```text
  Phase 1 (3v2)
     O  O  O
      \ | /
        D D
       [ G ]

  Phase 2 (4v3)  (new ball in immediately)
     O  O  O  O
       \ | | /
         D D D
        [ G ]

  Coaching cue: spacing first, then dodge/draw, then skip.
```

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: The 3v2

1. **The Attack:** The coach passes the ball to one of the three red offensive lines to start the 3v2 situation.
2. **The Play:** The three red players attack the two blue defenders. The offense focuses on spacing and drawing defenders, while the defense emphasizes communication and slow-playing the ball.
3. **Completion:** Phase 1 ends when a goal is scored, a shot is saved, or a turnover occurs.

### Phase 2: The 4v3

1. **The Switch:** Immediately after the 3v2 repetition ends, the coach throws a _second_ ball to the two blue lines.
2. **New Roles:**
    - The **three red players** who were just on offense immediately become the **three defenders**.
    - The **two blue defenders** from Phase 1 remain on the field and join the **two new blue players** to form a **four-player offense**.
3. **The Play:** The four blue players attack the three red defenders. Phase 2 ends when the play is dead (goal/save/turnover).

## 🧠 What Players Can Expect

- **Constant Role Switching 🔄:** Players must instantly shift their mindset from offense to defense (and vice-versa).
- **Strategic Spacing 📐:** Offense must set up (often in a box formation for 4v3) to create passing lanes and skip opportunities.
- **Communicative Defense 🗣️:** Defenders must talk constantly to manage the rotating disadvantage and "touch the hat" (the center of the field) during rotations.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- **Seamless Multi-Phase Play:** Players learn to navigate different odd-man scenarios in a single sequence.
- **Improved Ball Movement:** Offense learns to use skip passes and quick ball movement to break down a rotating defense.
- **Stout Disadvantaged Defense:** Defenders learn to recover to the middle, cover skip lanes, and delay the attack.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Slow Playing (Defense):** Defenders should not pick up the ball carrier until they are in a dangerous shooting/passing position.
- **Attack Topside (Offense from Low):** When starting from behind the goal, offensive players should try to attack topside to put pressure on the defense.
- **Cover Skip Lanes (Defense):** During 4v3 rotations, the defender moving to the next man should run through the middle of the field to disrupt skip passes.
- **Dodge and Slide (4v3):** In the 4v3 phase, use quick dodges to draw slides and create easy skip pass opportunities.

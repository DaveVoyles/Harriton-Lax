# 🥍 Riding Strategy

**Video Reference:** [Riding Reference Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bjIKPeCmhwc)

## 🎯 Overview

Riding is the first stage of defensive transition, occurring immediately after a turnover or a missed shot. This strategy focuses on preventing the opponent from clearing the ball past the midline by applying pressure, closing passing lanes, and forcing hurried decisions.

**Target Audience:** Attack, Midfield | **Focus Area:** Positioning, Persistence, Communication

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Immediate Pressure** — The closest player to the ball carrier prevents a clean outlet pass.
2. **Contain and Channel** — Force the clearer toward the sideline, away from midline outlets.
3. **Deny the Middle** — The most dangerous clearing lane is up the middle. Always take it away.
4. **Communication** — "Ball!" "I got 22!" "Push him sideline!"
5. **Hustle Rides** — Effort wins; ride hard for 5 seconds and most HS teams panic.

## ⚙️ Setup

Attack rides from their current positions — closest man pressures the ball, other two deny outlets. Midfielders hold the midline and prevent easy clears. LSM tracks the most athletic clearer.

## 🏃 Execution

1. **Phase 1: Ball Carrier Pressure** — Closest attackman sprints to the ball.
2. **Phase 2: Deny Outlets** — Other attackmen take away adjacent passing lanes.
3. **Phase 3: Midfield Support** — Middies pinch the sidelines if the ball advances.
4. **Phase 4: Recovery** — If the ball crosses midfield, recover and get back.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

Ride with purpose, not desperation. 5 hard seconds of pressure creates more turnovers than 15 seconds of lazy effort. Sprint to your man, then play smart.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Opponent fails to clear 2-3 times per game.
- Riding is a reliable possession-winning tool.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- Grade riding effort on film — reward hustle.
- Assign riding responsibilities before every game.
- Use "5-second ride" drill in practice.

---

_Note: Riding effort is a direct reflection of team culture. Set the standard early._

# 🥍 10-Man Ride Strategy

**Video Reference:** [Implementing a 10 Man Ride | POWLAX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bjIKPeCmhwc)

## 🎯 Overview

The 10-Man Ride is an aggressive, high-risk, high-reward riding strategy designed to pressure the clearing team by involving the goalie in the ride. By pushing the goalie out to cover an attackman, the riding team seeks to create a man-on-man situation across the field, forcing turnovers and preventing a clean break.

**Target Audience:** All Players (Midfielders, Attack, Defense, Goalie) | **Focus Area:** Aggression, Communication, Risk Management

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Goalie Involvement** — The goalie pushes out to cover an attackman, turning 9v10 into 10v10.
2. **Man-to-Man Pressure** — Every clearer is covered, no free outlets.
3. **Sideline Traps** — Force clearers toward the sideline where space is limited.
4. **Risk Management** — If the ride breaks, the offense has an empty net to shoot at.

## ⚙️ Setup

Each attackman and midfielder has a designated matchup. The goalie covers the deepest attackman. LSM plays the most athletic midfielder. Communication is critical — call out switches and slides.

## 🏃 Execution

1. **Phase 1:** On the whistle/turnover, the goalie sprints to the nearest attackman.
2. **Phase 2:** All riders match up man-to-man.
3. **Phase 3:** Force the ball carrier toward the sideline or back toward the end line.
4. **Phase 4:** If the clear is successful, sprint back to the hole — the goalie MUST get back to the cage.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

All-or-nothing mentality. If you commit, commit 100%. A half-effort 10-man ride gives up easy transition goals. The reward is a turnover in the offensive half.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Generate 1-2 turnovers per game via the 10-man ride.
- Opponent feels constant pressure on every clear attempt.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- Only use when up by 2+ or in desperation situations.
- Practice the goalie's sprint-back recovery religiously.
- Never use if your goalie can't handle the pressure.

---

_Note: The 10-man ride is a situational weapon — not an every-possession play. Use it wisely._

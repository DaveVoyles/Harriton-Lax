# ⚔️ Dead-Ball / Endline Plays

**Video Reference:** [Free Your Best Shooter: The Denver Endline Play Explained | POWLAX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NJsVF3oly7U)

## 🎯 Overview

Dead-ball restart plays executed after whistles — endline restarts, sideline restarts, and post-goal face-off plays. These are "free" possessions that can catch defenses off-guard.

**Target Audience:** Attack | **Focus Area:** Deception, Execution, Timing

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Speed of Restart** — The faster you restart, the less set the defense is.
2. **Deception** — Fake handoffs, misdirection, and dummy runners.
3. **Pre-Set Plays** — Everyone knows their role before the whistle.
4. **Existing Plays** — "Hidden Ball" endline and "Quick-Start" pick (detailed below).

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Endline Plays:** 3 attackmen near the restraining line, ball starts with the restart player on the end line.
- **Sideline Plays:** 2-man game set near the restart spot.

## 🏃 Execution

1. **Play 1 — Hidden Ball:** A-1 fakes handoff to A-2, defense chases A-2, A-1 attacks.
2. **Play 2 — Quick-Start Pick:** Immediate screen for the restart player to create a switch.
3. **Play 3 — Denver Endline:** A-1 passes to A-2 cutting across, A-3 sets a back pick, A-2 feeds the crease.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

Dead-ball plays are "bonus" possessions. Use them 2-3 times per game for maximum surprise. Over-use makes them predictable.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Score 1-2 goals per game from dead-ball plays.
- Opponent respects the restart and cannot relax after whistles.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- Practice dead-ball plays for 5 minutes per practice.
- Rotate which play you call based on scouting.
- The restart player must sell the fake.

_Note: Dead-ball plays are high-percentage scoring opportunities when rehearsed consistently._

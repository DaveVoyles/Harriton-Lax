# 🥍 Face-Off Strategy

**Video Reference:** [How To Be A Better Lacrosse Faceoff Specialist | Common FOGO Mistakes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FKXHWWjG8AI)

## 🎯 Overview

Comprehensive face-off strategy covering technique selection, wing play coordination, and situational adjustments. This document goes beyond individual drill work to outline an in-game system for winning the possession battle — from the whistle through the first pass in transition.

**Target Audience:** FOGO, Wings, Coaches
**Focus Area:** Possession Battle

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Read the Opponent:** Study the opposing FOGO's stance, hand position, and body lean before the whistle. Identify whether they favor a clamp, rake, or plunger — their dominant move will show itself in the first two face-offs. Track tendencies quarter by quarter and relay adjustments from the sideline.
2. **Counter System:** Every primary move has a counter. If they clamp, go over-the-top to pop the ball free. If they rake, jam their bottom hand and redirect the ball to your wing side. If they plunger, use a quick lateral push to disrupt their forward drive. The goal is to always have an answer — never take the same losing face-off twice.
3. **Wing Play Coordination:** Wings are not spectators — they decide 50/50 balls. Ball-side wing sprints to the expected exit point on the whistle. Off-side wing contains and cuts off the opponent's outlet lane. Communication calls ("Ball left!", "Push wide!") must be loud and immediate.
4. **Exit Strategy:** Winning the clamp means nothing without a clean exit. Primary options: go to hands and dodge if there is space, push wide to the sideline wing, or find the trailer (middie crashing from behind). Every face-off rep in practice must include the first two passes after possession.
5. **Backup FOGO Protocol:** When your primary FOGO is gassed or losing, have a defined backup ready. The backup should have a different style (e.g., if the primary is a clamp-first guy, the backup uses speed/rake). Switching can disrupt the opponent's rhythm and buy time for the primary to recover.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Pre-Game Scouting:** Review film or MaxPreps stats on the opponent's FOGO — win percentage, dominant hand, preferred technique. Share findings with your FOGO and wings before warm-ups.
- **Wristband Counter-Move Chart:** Provide the FOGO with a wristband card listing opponent tendencies and the corresponding counter move for quick sideline reference between face-offs.
- **Wing Positioning:** Choose staggered (one wing slightly ahead) for speed-based FOGOs who direct the ball to a specific side, or even stance for clamp-dominant FOGOs where the ball could squirt either direction.

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Standard Face-Off — Primary Move + Wing Sprint

1. FOGO sets up with their go-to primary technique (clamp, rake, or plunger).
2. On the whistle, execute the primary move with fast hands and low leverage.
3. Ball-side wing explodes to the ball on the whistle — do not wait to see who wins the clamp.
4. Off-side wing holds position to contain and prevent a fast break the other way.
5. If possession is won cleanly, outlet immediately — do not hold the ball at the X.

### Phase 2: Counter Face-Off — Adjust Based on Q1

1. After the first quarter, review face-off results on the sideline chart.
2. If the opponent's FOGO has shown a dominant move, call the specific counter from the wristband.
3. FOGO adjusts hand position and stance to set up the counter before the whistle.
4. Wings shift alignment based on where the ball is expected to exit (e.g., if countering a rake, ball will likely pop to the opposite side).

### Phase 3: Situational Face-Offs

1. **After a Goal (Quick Restart):** Be ready immediately — sprint to the X, set up fast, and catch the opponent off guard before they can substitute or set their wings.
2. **End-of-Quarter:** Possession to close a quarter is critical. Use your highest-percentage move — this is not the time to experiment.
3. **Must-Win Possessions:** In a tie game or down one, call timeout to set wings, review the counter chart, and give the FOGO a specific plan. Consider using the backup FOGO if the primary has been struggling.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Win the Clamp War 🔣:** The first two inches of hand speed off the whistle determine 80% of face-offs. Fast hands beat strong hands — train reaction time, not just grip strength.
- **Communication 🗣️:** Wings must call out ball direction instantly ("Left! Left!"). FOGO communicates pre-whistle adjustments with a simple code (e.g., "Fire" = going over-the-top, "Ice" = jamming).
- **Wing Effort Wins 50/50 Balls 📐:** When the clamp is contested and the ball is loose, the team with more aggressive wing play wins. Sprint through the ball, scoop and protect — every loose ball is a full-field ground ball mentality.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- 55%+ face-off possession rate across the game
- Clean exits to offense — ball moved out of the defensive half within 5 seconds of winning the draw
- No turnovers at the X from poor exits or indecision after winning possession

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Chart Every Face-Off Result:** Track win/loss, technique used, and which side the ball exited. Use this data to make real-time adjustments and prepare for rematches.
- **Rotate Wings for Fresh Legs:** Face-off wings sprint on every whistle — fatigue leads to lost ground balls. Sub wings every 3–4 face-offs to keep intensity high.
- **Call Timeout if Losing the X Battle 0–4:** If the FOGO starts 0-for-4, burn a timeout to reset. Switch to the backup, change the wing alignment, or shift to a new counter. Do not let a losing streak extend unchecked.

## 🎯 Overview

22 Cat is a variation of the 2-2-2 offensive set that focuses on circular motion and "wheel" actions. It is designed to disrupt man-to-man defenses by constantly rotating players through different scoring zones.

**Target Audience:** Attack, Midfield
**Focus Area:** Motion Offense, Rotation, Defensive Confusion

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. Continuous Motion: Players are rarely static; the set relies on constant cycling between positions.
2. Wheel Action: A specific rotation where players move in a circular pattern to replace each other's spots.
3. Misdirection: Using cuts and picks to hide the intended target of the play.

## ⚙️ Offensive Setup

- Personnel: Standard 3 Attack, 3 Midfield.
- Initial Alignment: Similar to 22 Dog, starting in a 2-2-2 box formation.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: The Wheel

1. On the signal, the players at X rotate to the wings, while wings move to the top, and top players cycle down to X.

### Phase 2: The Strike

1. As the defense adjusts to the rotation, a designated cutter breaks toward the crease for a feed.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- Flow State: Maintain the rhythm of the rotation without stalling.
- Awareness: Recognize when a defender is caught out of position during the cycle.
- Timing: The pass must be made exactly as the cutter enters the window.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Defensive breakdown due to missed assignments during rotation.
- Uncontested shots from the crease or the hole.
- Maintaining possession while putting constant pressure on the defense.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- Finish the Cut: Players must follow through with their rotations even if they don't receive the ball.
- Communicate the Cycle: Use a verbal cue to initiate the movement.

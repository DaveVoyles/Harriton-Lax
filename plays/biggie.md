## 🎯 Overview

Biggie is a heavy Big-Little pick set designed to exploit defensive mismatches, specifically targeting short-stick defenders by involving them in 2-man actions with primary dodgers.

**Target Audience:** Attack, Midfield
**Focus Area:** Mismatches, Big-Little Picks, Isolation

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. Big-Little Pick: An attackman (Big) sets a pick for a midfielder (Little) or vice versa to force a switch between a long-pole and a short-stick.
2. Isolation (ISO): After the switch, the ball carrier isolates the weaker defender.
3. Crease Crowding: Using the picker to seal the defender and create a lane to the goal.

## ⚙️ Offensive Setup

- Location: Typically run from the top-center or wings.
- Personnel: Requires one strong dodger and one solid picker.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: The Setup

1. The dodger brings the ball to the top, signaling the Biggie action.
2. The picker moves to the high-side of the on-ball defender.

### Phase 2: The Switch

1. The dodger uses the pick to force the defenders to either switch or hedge.
2. If they switch, the dodger attacks the short-stick defender immediately.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- Aggression: Attack the mismatch with confidence.
- Recognition: Quickly identify if the defense switched or stayed.
- Spacing: Keep the backside of the field clear to prevent help defense.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Forcing a short-stick midfielder to defend a primary dodging attackman.
- Creating a free lane to the goal if the defense fails to communicate the switch.
- Drawing a slide from the crease to open up backside shooters.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- Solid Base: The picker must be stationary and have a wide base.
- Attack the Hip: The dodger must drive off the picker's hip to eliminate space for the defender.

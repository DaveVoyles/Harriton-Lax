# 🥍 22 Dog

**Video Reference:** [22 Dog](https://youtu.be/jyjN1uwod2g)

## 🎯 Overview

22 Dog is a settled offensive set designed to create high-percentage scoring opportunities through a 2-2-2 formation. It emphasizes spacing and quick ball movement to isolate defenders and create 2-man game advantages.

**Target Audience:** Attack, Midfield
**Focus Area:** Spacing, 2-Man Game, Ball Movement

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **2-2-2 Formation:** Two players at X, two on the wings, and two up top. This spreads the defense and creates clear lanes for dodging and feeding.
2. **Pairs Coordination:** Players work in pairs (top, wing, X) to set picks and create mismatches.
3. **Quick Reversal:** Rapidly moving the ball from one side of the field to the other to force the defense to shift and miss rotations.

## ⚙️ Offensive Setup

- **X (Behind Goal):** Two attackmen positioned behind the cage to initiate play and feed.
- **Wings:** Two offensive players (typically one attack, one midfield) on the goal line extended (GLE) or slightly above.
- **Top:** Two midfielders positioned up top to provide shooting threats and safety valves.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: Initiation

1. The ball starts at X or up top. The initiator looks to draw a slide or look for a 2-man game with their partner.

### Phase 2: The Action

1. The pair on the ball side executes a pick-and-roll or a brush pick.
2. The off-ball pairs maintain spacing and look for skip pass opportunities.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Patience ⏱️:** Wait for the pick to set properly before making the move.
- **Communication 🗣️:** Call out picks and "one-more" passes clearly.
- **Spacing 📐:** Occupy the defense across the entire offensive zone to prevent easy slides.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Creating 2v1 advantages in high-scoring areas.
- Forcing defensive switches and mismatches.
- High-percentage shots from the crease or wings.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Shoulder-to-Shoulder:** Ensure the dodger brushes shoulders with the picker.
- **Eyes Up:** Feeders at X must keep their eyes on the crease for cutters.

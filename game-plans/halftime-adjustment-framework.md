# 🥍 Halftime Adjustment Framework

**Video Reference:** [How To Lead A Sharp, Prepared Team Every Game | POWLAX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AdnZS-2u8GY)

## 🎯 Overview

A structured 10-minute halftime framework for diagnosing problems, selecting adjustments, and communicating changes to the team. Halftime is the most important 10 minutes in any game — it must be organized, data-driven, and calm. This framework ensures coaches diagnose before prescribing and players leave halftime with clear, actionable focus points.

**Target Audience:** Coaching Staff
**Focus Area:** Game Management, Communication

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **The 3-2-3-2 Framework:** Structure the 10-minute halftime into four blocks — 3 minutes for diagnosis, 2 minutes for decision-making, 3 minutes for communication, and 2 minutes for warm-up and mental reset. This prevents the common mistake of spending all 10 minutes talking without a plan.
2. **Data-Driven Diagnosis:** Use concrete stats to identify issues — shots on goal, ground balls won/lost, clear percentage, turnovers, and face-off win rate. Opinions are valuable, but data removes bias and focuses the conversation on what actually happened.
3. **Maximum 2 Adjustments Per Half:** Players cannot absorb more than 2 tactical changes at halftime. Identify the top 2 issues causing the most damage and address only those. Everything else waits for the next practice.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **Halftime Checklist (Printed):** Every coach should carry a printed halftime checklist with sections for stats, observations, adjustments selected, and communication assignments. Do not rely on memory.
- **Stat Sheet Template:** Assign a stat keeper (manager or JV coach) to track shots, ground balls, clears, turnovers, and face-offs in real time. The stat sheet should be handed to the head coach immediately at halftime.
- **Captain Communication Roles:** Assign each captain a position group to relay information to — one for attack, one for midfield, one for defense. Captains are the bridge between coaches and players.
- **Position Group Breakouts:** After the coaching staff makes decisions, break the team into position groups (attack, midfield, defense, goalies) for targeted communication before bringing everyone back together.

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Gather Data and Observe (First 3 Minutes)

1. Coaches review the stat sheet and identify the top 2-3 statistical issues (e.g., "We are 2-for-8 on clears" or "They have 6 ground balls to our 2").
2. Each positional coach shares their top observation — what is working and what is not.
3. Head coach listens, synthesizes, and identifies the 2 most impactful issues to address.

### Phase 2: Select Adjustments (2 Minutes)

1. Reference the [Game Adjustments Playbook](adjustments-playbook.md) for pre-built tactical solutions.
2. Select no more than 2 adjustments — one offensive and one defensive, or both on the same side of the ball if the issue is concentrated.
3. Frame each adjustment as a simple, specific action: "We are going to..." not "We need to be better at..."

### Phase 3: Communicate to Team (3 Minutes)

1. Head coach addresses the full team first: acknowledge effort, state the 2 adjustments clearly.
2. Break into position groups — captains and positional coaches reinforce the adjustments with specific instructions for each group.
3. Bring the team back together for a final 30-second energy moment — a cheer, a rally, a focus word.

### Phase 4: Mental Reset and Warm-Up (2 Minutes)

1. Players hydrate and mentally reset — encourage deep breaths and positive visualization.
2. Light stick work and passing to get hands and feet moving before the second half begins.
3. Captains do a final "echo check" — ask 2-3 players to repeat the adjustments to ensure the message landed.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Stay Calm 🧊:** The coaching staff sets the emotional tone. If coaches are calm, players will be calm. Never panic, never blame, never raise your voice in frustration at halftime.
- **Diagnose Before Prescribing 🔍:** The biggest halftime mistake is jumping to solutions before understanding the problem. Spend the first 3 minutes listening and reading data, not talking.
- **Trust the Process 🔗:** The 3-2-3-2 framework works because it is repeatable. The same routine every game builds confidence and eliminates chaos.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Coaches have a repeatable, structured halftime routine that maximizes the 10-minute window.
- Adjustments are targeted, specific, and limited to 2 per half so players can absorb and execute them.
- Players leave halftime with 1-2 clear focus points and renewed energy.
- The team shows measurable improvement in the second half on the identified issues.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Print the Checklist:** Carry the halftime checklist for every game. Preparation eliminates scrambling.
- **Assign a Stat Keeper:** Without data, halftime diagnosis is guesswork. A dedicated stat keeper is non-negotiable.
- **Rehearse in Practice:** Run a mock halftime during a mid-week practice scrimmage. Stop at the halfway point, run the full 3-2-3-2 routine, and then continue. This makes the real thing feel familiar.
- **Common Mistake:** Coaches try to fix everything at halftime. Resist the urge — pick the top 2 issues and let the rest go until the next practice.

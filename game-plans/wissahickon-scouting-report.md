# 🥍 Pre-Game Scouting Report: Wissahickon Playday

**📅 Date:** March 7, 2026 — 10:00 AM
**🏟️ Location:** Wissahickon High School (Away)
**📌 Event Type:** Playday / Jamboree (multi-team scrimmage format)

## 🎯 Overview

The Wissahickon Playday is Harriton's **2026 season opener** — a jamboree-style event featuring shortened scrimmages against multiple opponents. Because pre-season film on opponents is unavailable, this report shifts focus from opposition-specific breakdowns to **our own execution priorities**. The playday serves as a live evaluation environment: every possession is a chance to assess player readiness, system execution, and conditioning under game-speed conditions.

Coaches should treat each period as a controlled test of a specific system element (settled offense, defensive slides, transition, special teams). The goal is not just to win reps — it is to **gather actionable data** that shapes the first week of regular-season preparation.

**Target Audience:** Coaching Staff
**Focus Area:** Self-Scouting, Live System Evaluation, Player Assessment

---

## 💡 Key Concepts

### What to Evaluate During the Playday

1. **Player Fitness & Conditioning:** Are players sustaining effort through the final period? Note any visible drop-off in sprint speed, ground ball hustle, or defensive recovery.
2. **Communication Quality:** Is the defense talking — calling slides, ball position, and "hot" calls? Are middies communicating ride/clear assignments? Reference the [Communication Glossary](../defense/communication-glossary.md) as the standard.
3. **Ride & Clear Execution:** How cleanly are we clearing under pressure? Are riders forcing turnovers or letting the ball advance uncontested? See [Clearing Strategy](../rides-and-clears/clearing.md) and [Riding Strategy](../rides-and-clears/riding.md).
4. **System Compliance:** Are players running the schemes as installed, or freelancing? Early-season habits set the tone for the year.

### Key Performance Indicators by Unit

| Unit | KPI | Target |
|------|-----|--------|
| **Offense** | Shots on goal percentage | 60%+ |
| **Offense** | Unforced turnovers per period | ≤ 2 |

## ⚙️ Tactical Setup

### Our Offensive Game Plan

The offensive approach progresses from simple to complex across the playday, building rhythm before testing advanced concepts.

- **Open with Simple Motion:** Start with basic [settled offensive sets](../offense/README.md) to establish spacing discipline and ball movement tempo. Get every attackman and middie a touch early.
- **Test the 2-Man Game Early:** Deploy the [2-Man Game](../offense/mastering-the-2-man-game.md) (pick-and-roll) within the first two possessions of each scrimmage. This immediately reveals the opponent's slide package — do they hard hedge, switch, or trail?
- **Push Transition Aggressively:** Every defensive stop or face-off win should trigger an immediate outlet. Use transition opportunities to evaluate conditioning and decision-making at speed. Run the [3-Man Weave](../drills/3-man-weave.md) concept in live play.
- **Zone Beaters on Standby:** If any opponent shows a zone look, shift to the [Zone Beater](../offense/how-to-beat-zone-defense.md) package — high post touches, skip passes, and crease feeds.

### Our Defensive Game Plan

- **Default: Ram Defense** — Start every scrimmage in our base [Ram Defense](../defense/ram-defense.md) with the standard [crease slide package](../defense/sliding-and-recovery.md). See the full [Ram Defensive Principles](../defense/ram-defensive-principles.md) manual for rules and rotations.
- **Evaluate Communication Between Slides:** Coaches on the sideline should specifically listen for and grade verbal calls during slides. Are adjacent defenders calling "I'm two!" or staying silent? Reference the [Communication Glossary](../defense/communication-glossary.md).
- **Test 3-3 Zone (At Least One Period):** Dedicate at least one scrimmage period to the [3-3 Zone Defense](../defense/3-3-zone.md). This is a live rep opportunity that cannot be replicated in practice. Evaluate top-row pressure and bottom-row rotation speed.
- **On-Ball Fundamentals:** Use the [On-Ball Defense Mechanics](../defense/on-ball-defense-mechanics.md) and [Defensive Approach Angles](../defense/defensive-approach-angles.md) as the evaluation checklist for individual defenders.

### Special Teams Focus

All special teams schemes follow the [Special Teams Strategy](../plays/special-teams.md) playbook.

- **Face-offs:**
  - Start with the **clamp** as the primary move to establish early possession.
  - Vary technique (rake, plunger) based on what the opposing FOGO shows.
  - Wings must execute per the [Face-off Wing Play](../plays/fundamentals/face-off-wing-play.md) guide — ground ball positioning and box-out technique are the priority.
- **EMO (Man-Up Offense):**
  - Run the primary EMO set from the [Special Teams doc](../plays/special-teams.md).
  - Emphasis on ball movement speed — the extra pass creates the open look.
  - Track shot selection: are we shooting from high-percentage areas?
- **MDD (Man-Down Defense):**
  - Run the [5-Man Rotation](../defense/man-down/5-man-rotation.md) as the base.
  - Focus on protecting the "house" (crease and near-crease area).
  - Communicate top-side pressure and deny skip passes.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Period 1: Establish the Foundation

1. Run base Ram Defense — no exotic looks.
2. Offense runs settled sets with simple motion.
3. Coaches evaluate spacing, timing, and individual fundamentals.

### Period 2: Test Systems Under Pressure

1. Introduce 2-man game on offense.
2. Switch to 3-3 zone on defense for the full period.
3. Push transition at every opportunity — evaluate outlet passing and fill lanes.

### Period 3: Special Teams & Conditioning Check

1. Prioritize EMO and MDD reps — call timeouts to set up special teams situations if needed.
2. Evaluate face-off adjustments based on Period 1–2 results.
3. Monitor 4th-quarter conditioning: who is still sprinting, who is jogging?

### Period 4: Full Integration

1. Run the complete system — offense, defense, transition, and special teams as a cohesive unit.
2. Make in-game adjustments based on what was observed in earlier periods (see [Adjustments Playbook](adjustments-playbook.md)).
3. Final evaluation window — players are competing for starting roles.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Process Over Outcome 🔣:** The scoreboard does not matter at a playday. What matters is *how* we execute. A well-run possession that doesn't score is more valuable than a broken play that gets lucky.
- **Communication 🗣️:** Every player should be talking — on defense ("Ball! Ball! Ball!", "I'm hot!", "Two slide!"), on rides ("Back!", "Dead side!"), and on clears ("Break!", "Release!"). Silence is a failure.
- **Compete on Every Ground Ball 📐:** Ground balls are the purest conditioning and effort test. Track them by player. The effort level here predicts regular-season toughness. Reference [Ground Ball Mechanics](../drills/stick-work/ground-ball-mechanics.md) for technique reminders.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Every installed offensive set gets live reps (settled motion, 2-man game, zone beater, transition).
- Ram Defense and 3-3 Zone both receive at least one full period of game-speed evaluation.
- Special teams (face-offs, EMO, MDD) are tested and baseline data is captured.
- Coaching staff completes the Player Evaluation Checklist (below) for every rostered player.
- At least 3 actionable priorities for Week 1 practice are identified from playday observations.

---

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **Rotate Liberally:** This is an evaluation event. Every player on the roster should get meaningful minutes. Stagger substitutions to see different unit combinations.
- **Sideline Coaching Is Teaching:** Use stoppages and between-period huddles to coach in real time. Point out specific examples — "That slide was late because you didn't hear the call. Let's fix that."
- **Assign Evaluation Roles:** Each assistant coach should be responsible for grading a specific unit (e.g., Coach A grades defense, Coach B grades offense, Coach C grades special teams) to ensure complete coverage.
- **Film Everything:** If possible, record every scrimmage period. Film review in the following week is where the real scouting value lives.

# 🛡️ Close Defense Mastery

## 🎯 Overview

Close defense mastery is the foundation of a championship-caliber defensive unit. This guide covers crease defense fundamentals, GLE discipline, body positioning against crease attackmen, handling feeds and inside finishes, and communication with the goalie. A great close defender takes away time, space, and angles — not just the ball.

**Target Audience:** Defense
**Focus Area:** Crease Defense, GLE Discipline, Communication

---

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Body Positioning Over Stick Checks:** The best close defenders use their body to deny position before ever throwing a check. Chest on hands, hips low, feet active. A check is the last resort, not the first.
2. **GLE Awareness:** Goal Line Extended is the line of no return. Any attacker who gets below GLE with their hands free has a direct angle to the goal. Defenders must force dodgers above GLE or seal them below with a dead angle.
3. **Crease Ownership:** The crease area is sacred ground. Defenders must physically deny attackmen from establishing position on the crease. This means constant hand fighting, stick pressure on gloves, and inside body positioning.
4. **Communication with the Goalie:** The close defender and the goalie work as a single unit. The goalie sees the whole field; the defender trusts the goalie's calls for positioning, approach angles, and shot readiness.
5. **Front-Side / Back-Side Discipline:** Defenders must understand when they are front-side (between ball and man) versus back-side (away from ball). Front-side defenders stay tight; back-side defenders sag toward the hole and help.

---

## ⚙️ Defensive Setup

- **Crease Defender:** Positioned between the crease attackman and the ball at all times. Inside foot and inside hand deny the passing lane. Never let the attackman get topside position on the crease.
- **GLE Defender:** When guarding an attackman at or below GLE, stay on the topside hip. Force everything toward the end line where the angle dies. Hands are high to disrupt feeds.
- **Adjacent Defender:** The defender one pass away. Must be in a "two feet in the paint" help position — close enough to slide but connected enough to recover to their man.
- **Backside Defender:** The defender farthest from the ball. Sags into the hole and becomes the third slide. Eyes on both man and ball — never lose sight of either.

---

## 🏃 Execution

### Phase 1: Basic Positioning (Half Speed)

1. Walk through crease positioning with no ball. Defender practices maintaining inside position as the attackman moves around the crease arc.
2. Add a stationary ball at X. Defender adjusts body angle to stay between man and ball. Coach checks hand position and foot placement.
3. Introduce a feed from X. Defender works on getting hands on the attackman's gloves and disrupting the catch without fouling.

### Phase 2: Live Crease Defense (3v3)

1. Three defenders guard three attackmen in a half-field setting. One attackman is designated as the crease player.
2. Ball starts at X and moves around the horn. Defenders must communicate and adjust positioning with each pass.
3. Emphasis on the crease defender: deny the catch, disrupt the finish, and communicate "I've got crease!" or "Check crease!" on every possession.

### Phase 3: Full 6v6 Integration

1. Add midfielders and run full 6v6 settled offense vs. defense.
2. Defenders execute the full slide package while maintaining crease discipline. The crease defender only leaves if they are the designated hot slide.
3. Goalie actively directs positioning with verbal calls. Defenders respond and adjust in real time.

---

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Physicality 💪:** Crease defense is a contact position. Embrace the physical battle. Win the positioning war before the ball arrives, not after.
- **Communication 🗣️:** Never stop talking. Calls include: "Ball!" (I have on-ball), "One more!" (I'm one pass away), "Two!" (I'm the second slide), "Crease!" (I see the crease threat), and "Fire!" (slide now).
- **Anticipation 🧠:** Read the passer's eyes and stick angle. A great crease defender jumps the passing lane without gambling — they cheat a half-step toward the feed before it's thrown.
- **Recovery 📐:** When beaten, sprint through the hole (center of the field in front of the crease). Never trail behind — go through the middle and re-establish inside position.

---

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Crease defenders consistently deny inside position and disrupt feeds
- Defenders maintain GLE discipline and force attackers into dead-angle shots
- Communication between close defenders and the goalie is constant and clear
- Slide responsibilities are executed without leaving the crease unguarded
- Low-angle and crease-area shot percentage drops significantly

---

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **"Hands before feet":** When an attackman receives a feed on the crease, the defender's first reaction should be a stick check on the hands — not a body check. Disrupt the finish.
- **"Top-side or dead-side":** Below GLE, there are only two acceptable positions: on the attacker's topside hip (denying the turn) or forcing them to the dead-side behind the goal with no angle. Never get stuck in the middle.
- **"Don't chase behind":** When an attackman moves behind the cage, do not chase. Set up on the topside and let the goalie direct you. The attacker must come to you.
- **"Play through the hands":** Constant stick pressure on the attackman's bottom hand and gloves. This is legal, effective, and demoralizing. Keep your stick in their kitchen.
- **"Eyes up, hips low":** Maintain an athletic stance at all times. Hips low for leverage, eyes up to see the ball and the field. Never stand straight up on the crease.

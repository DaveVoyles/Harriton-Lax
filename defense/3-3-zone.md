# 🥍 3-3 Zone Defense

**Video Reference:** [3-3 Zone Defense Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CA4P12dy7S4)

## 🎯 Overview

The 3-3 Zone is a high-pressure defensive system designed to disrupt the opponent's rhythm and force turnovers by packing the middle and pressuring the perimeter. It involves three defenders playing low near the crease and three defenders playing high to cover the top of the box.

**Target Audience:** Defense, Midfield, Goalies
**Focus Area:** Spacing, Communication, Perimeter Pressure

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Layered Coverage:** The defensive alignment creates two layers (Low 3 and High 3), making it difficult for the offense to find easy passing lanes through the middle.
2. **Inside-Out Pressure:** Defenders stay compact to protect the crease, then expand to pressure the ball carrier when they enter their designated area.
3. **Communication is Key:** Constant talk between the high and low layers is essential for passing off offensive players as they move through the zones.

## ⚙️ Setup

- **The Low 3:** Three defenders positioned near the crease and GLE (Goal Line Extended) to protect the 'hole'.
- **The High 3:** Three defenders (usually midfielders) positioned at the top of the box to pressure outside shooters and dodgers.
- **Goalie Role:** Acts as the 'director', calling out slides and identifying threats from both layers.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: Zone Establishment

1. Defensive players quickly find their areas (Low Left, Low Crease, Low Right / High Left, High Point, High Right).
2. Establish a 'picket fence' look at the top to deter outside shots.

### Phase 2: Rotation and Sliding

1. As the ball moves, the zone shifts as a unit, maintaining the 3-3 structure relative to the ball position.
2. Slides are typically 'adjacent', with the nearest defender in the layer providing the first look.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Discipline 🔣:** Players must stay in their zones. Chasing the ball outside of the area breaks the integrity of the 3-3.
- **Communication 🗣️:** 'Stay High!', 'I have got low crease!', 'Ball is at point!'
- **Spacing 📐:** Maintain enough distance to cover the passing lanes between the two layers.

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Reduced high-percentage shots from the crease area.
- Forced outside shots from distance.
- Increased turnovers due to disrupted offensive flow.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- Drill the 'layer transition' where the offense moves from high to low to test the hand-off between defenders.
- Emphasize stick positioning to take away 'skip passes' through the 3-3 layers.
- Ensure defenders understand they aren't 'chasing' but 'occupying' space relative to the ball.

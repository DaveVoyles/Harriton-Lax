# 🧤 Goalkeeper Mastery & Fundamentals

This document consolidates our goalie training protocols, save mechanics, and fundamental drills.

**Video Reference:** [Goalie Play Basics (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7i1pPgYV_J8)

## 🎯 Overview

Build consistent goalie fundamentals: ready stance, angle play, stepping to the ball, rebound control, and communication. High-level training focuses on reaction time, footwork, and clearing accuracy.

**Focus Area:** Save Mechanics, Footwork, Clearing, Communication

---

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **Feet First, Hands Second:** Goalies make saves with feet first—step and drive the head of the stick to the ball.
2. **Angle & Arc Discipline:** Stay centered to the shooter using an arc relative to the ball. Better angles make the goalie look bigger and the net looks smaller.
3. **Rebound Control:** Turning saves into possessions by securing the ball immediately.
4. **Defensive General:** Your voice runs the defense. Be loud and proactive (identify ball, cutters, slides, and outlets).

---

## 🏹 Save Mechanics & Training

### 🏋️ Core Skills

- **Step to the Ball:** Always step 45 degrees toward the shot. Head to the ball.
- **Top Hand Primary:** The top hand drives the save; the bottom hand provides stability.
- **Eyes on the Pocket:** Track the ball all the way into the mesh.
- **Reaction Drills:** Use tennis balls for faster reaction training to improve hand-eye coordination.
- **Stick Work:** Utilize the [Elite Wall Ball Routine](../drills/stick-work/elite-wall-ball-routine.md) for goalie-specific top-hand and cross-body practice.

## ⚙️ Practical Drills

### 🗺️ Visual Map: Angle + Arc in the Crease

```text
                  (shooter up top)
                           O
                           |
                           |
       left pipe  [===== G =====]  right pipe
                         \    |    /
                           \   |   /
                            \  |  /
                              \ | /
                               \|/

   Goalie moves on an arc to stay centered on the ball.
   Cue: Square shoulders and hips to the shooter.
```

### 🏃 Execution Steps

1. **Ready Stance Check (10 reps):** Feet wider than shoulders, knees bent, chest tall. Hands up and in front; stick head centered.
2. **Angle/Arc Movement (10 reps each side):** Shooter walks the ball wing → top → wing. Goalie shuffles on the arc, staying square.
3. **Step-to-Ball Saves (3 sets x 6 shots):** Shooter places shots (body/hip, then off-hip/high). Goalie calls “SHOT!”, steps lead foot, and attacks with the stick.
4. **Rebound Control + Outlet (10 reps):** Save → secure ("cover") → scan → communicate find an outlet.

---

## 🛡️ Clearing & Strategy

### 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **"Next Rep" Mentality:** Reset immediately after every shot, regardless of the outcome.
- **Proactive Communication:** Be loud early, not late. Proactive talk prevents defensive breakdowns.

### 🏹 Clearing Logic

- **Immediate Release:** Clear to the wings before the attack can establish a ride.
- **Quarterback Mentality:** Call out slides and offensive positioning during the clear to guide the transition.

---

## ✅ Desired Outcomes

- Improved save percentage through better positional discipline and stepping mechanics.
- Fewer "all-hands" saves and better cushioning (soft hands).
- Faster transition and more possessions via rebound control and efficient outlets.

## 📋 Coaching Points

- **"Feet first!"** and **"Head to the ball!"**
- **If hands drop:** Pause and reset stance before continuing.
- **If rebound pops out:** Emphasize bringing the ball to the body immediately.

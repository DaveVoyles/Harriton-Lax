# 🥍 Defensive Approach Angles & On-Ball Positioning

**Video Reference:** [Defensive Approach Angles & On-Ball Mechanics](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OQN-m20q8Lw)

## 🎯 Overview

The "approach" is the most critical second in a defensive possession. This strategy focuses on the geometry of closing space, establishing a topside seal, and maintaining the proper distance to neutralize dodgers before they reach high-percentage scoring areas.

**Target Audience:** Close Defense, LSMs, SSDMs
**Focus Area:** Footwork, Angular Geometry, Stick Positioning

## 💡 Key Concepts

1. **The "Banana" Approach:** Avoiding a straight-line sprint that allows the dodger to blow by. Instead, approach on a slight curve to "seal the topside" and force the dodger down the alley.
2. **Short, Choppy Steps:** Transitioning from a full sprint to controlled, balanced footwork as you enter the dodger's "impact zone."
3. **The "Poke & Retract":** Using the stick to gauge distance and disrupt rhythm without over-committing your body weight.

## ⚙️ Tactical Setup

- **Body Stance:** Hips low, chest tall, and "hands out" to initiate early contact.
- **Lead Foot:** Establishing a "high foot" to block the topside lane and dictate the dodge direction.
- **The "Punch":** Using the bottom hand to apply pressure to the dodger's hip once contact is made.

## 🏃 Execution Principles

### Phase 1: Closing Space
- Recognize the ball carrier's movement and sprint to within 5 yards.
- Begin the "breakdown" (short steps) to prepare for the dodge.

### Phase 2: Establishing the Seal
- Position your body "topside" of the dodger.
- Force the dodger's path toward the sideline or end-line, minimizing their shooting angle.

### Phase 3: Engagement & Steering
- Maintain a stick-length of distance until the dodger commits to a direction.
- Once the dodger is in the "alley," use your hands and hips to steer them away from the middle.

## 🧠 Strategic Mindset

- **Patience 🧘:** Do not lunge. Lunging is the result of poor approach angles.
- **Discipline ⚖️:** "Feet first, hands second." If your feet are out of position, your stick won't save you.
- **Communication 🗣️:** "I've got ball!", "I'm topside!", "Drive him down!"

## ✅ Outcomes

- Neutralization of primary dodge threats without requiring sliding.
- Forced low-angle shots from the wings and alleys.
- Improved defensive stability and fewer "on-ball" breakdowns.

## 📋 Coaching

- Drill the "Approach to X" where defenders must sprint to a cone and breakdown in a proper stance.
- Use "Topside Seals" 1v1 drills focusing only on the first 3 steps of a dodge.
- Cue: "Seal the middle, steer to the alley."
